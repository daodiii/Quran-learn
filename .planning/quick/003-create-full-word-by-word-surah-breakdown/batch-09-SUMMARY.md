## Surah-Specific Analysis Notes

**Thematic Structure:**
1. **Verses 1-10:** The incident (Prophet ﷺ and the blind man) — moral lesson on valuing sincere seekers
2. **Verses 11-16:** The Quran's nature (honored scriptures in hands of noble angels)
3. **Verses 17-23:** Human ingratitude despite creation blessings
4. **Verses 24-32:** Agricultural provisions (water, earth, crops) as signs
5. **Verses 33-42:** Day of Judgment (fleeing from family, two types of faces)

**Key Grammatical Features for Advanced Learners:**
- **Voice shift:** 3rd person narrative opening (ʿabasa, tawallā) transitioning to direct 2nd person address (yudrīka, anta), creating rhetorical engagement
- **Conditional contrasts:** *Ammā man istaghná... fa-anta lahu taṣaddā* vs *Ammā man jāʾaka yasʿā... fa-anta ʿanhu talahhā* (parallel structures with opposite meanings)
- **Creation sequence:** Form I, II, and IV verbs showing stages (khalaqahu → qaddarahu → yassarahu → amātahu → aqbarahu → ansharahu)
- **Agricultural sequence:** Causative Form IV *anbatnā* governing long list of coordinated accusative objects (ḥabban, ʿinaban, qaḍban, etc.)
- **Eschatological descriptions:** Contrast structures (bright faces vs dust-covered faces) using active participles as predicates
