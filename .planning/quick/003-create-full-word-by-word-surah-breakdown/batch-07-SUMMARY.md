## Advanced Grammar Features Covered

### At-Takwir (81)
- **Conditional chains:** 12 consecutive idha clauses (vv.1-13) with single delayed apodosis (v.14)
- **Passive voice dominance:** kuwwirat, inkadarat, suyyirat, 'uttilat, hushirat, sujjirat, zuwwijat
- **Passive interrogative:** su'ilat (v.8) - the buried infant is asked
- **Oath sequences:** fa-la uqsimu bi-al-khunnasi (vv.15-18) triple oath structure
- **Emphasis particles:** wa-la-qad (v.23), ma...bi negation (vv.22,24,25)
- **Divine attributes:** Multiple na't (adjectives) for Gabriel (vv.19-21)
- **Restriction:** in...illa hasr pattern (v.27)
- **Masdar mu'awwal:** an yastaqima (v.28), an yasha'a (v.29)

### Al-Inshiqaq (84)
- **Conditional structures:** idha...wa-adhinat li-rabbiha wa-huqqat (vv.1-5)
- **Vocative:** ya ayyuha al-insan (v.6) formal address
- **Amma conditionals:** fa-amma man utiya kitabahu bi-yaminihi...fa- (vv.7-9, 10-12)
- **Oath formulas:** fa-la uqsimu bi-ash-shafaqi (vv.16-18)
- **Energetic mood:** la-tarkabunna (v.19) oath response with nun of emphasis
- **Circumstantial (hal):** masruran (vv.9,13) describing state
- **Inna + kana:** Compound emphasis structures (vv.13,15)
- **Ironic rhetoric:** bashshirhum bi-'adhabin (v.24) - "good news" of punishment
- **Exception:** illa alladhina amanu (v.25)

### Al-Ghashiyah (88)
- **Interrogative hal:** Rhetorical opening (v.1) creating suspense
- **Nominal descriptive sentences:** wujuhun khashi'atun 'amilatun nasibatun (vv.2-3)
- **Multiple predicates:** Single subject with 3-4 khabar (vv.2-4, 8-16)
- **Active participles:** khashi'ah, 'amilah, nasibah, hamiyah, na'imah, radiyah, jariyah
- **Passive participles:** marfu'ah, mawdu'ah, masfufah, mabthuthah
- **Inverted nominal sentences:** fiha 'aynun jariyah (vv.12-16) - predicate before subject
- **A-fa-la interrogative:** a-fa-la yanzuruna (v.17) rhetorical challenge
- **Ellipsis:** Parallel structures (vv.18-20) with omitted verb
- **Restriction:** innama anta mudhakkirun (v.21)
- **Emphatic negation:** lasta 'alayhim bi-musaytirin (v.22) - laysa + bi
- **Inna inversion:** inna ilayna iyabahum (vv.25-26) - predicate before subject

## Morphological Patterns

**Form VII (infa'ala) - Reflexive/Passive:**
- inkadarat (81:2) - stars scattered themselves
- inshaqqat (84:1) - sky split itself
- yanqalibu (84:9) - he returns/turns himself

**Form II Passive (fu''ilat) - Intensive Passive:**
- kuwwirat (81:1) - sun wrapped up intensely
- suyyirat (81:3) - mountains moved with force
- sujjirat (81:6) - seas set ablaze intensely
- su''irat (81:12) - Hellfire blazed intensely

**Form IV (af'ala) - Causative:**
- uzlifat (81:13) - brought near
- utiya (84:7,10) - was given (passive)
- yusminu, yughni (88:7) - fattens, satisfies

**Active Participles as Predicates:**
- kadihun (84:6) - laboring
- mudhakkirun (88:21) - one who reminds
- musaytir (88:22) - controller
