- Context: "You've analyzed individual surahs in detail (L5.02-05). Now zoom out — look ACROSS surahs to find PATTERNS. Juz 'Amma's short surahs share recognizable grammatical structures. Learning to spot these patterns accelerates your Quranic reading."

**4-Step Pattern Recognition Method (from 16-RESEARCH.md Pattern 3):**
Step 1: Identify the pattern — What grammatical structure repeats?
Step 2: Catalog examples — Where does this pattern appear?
Step 3: Analyze function — WHY is this pattern used in these contexts?
Step 4: Compare variations — How does the pattern adapt?

**Section 2 — Pattern 1: Oath Formulas (Introduction):**
- Briefly introduce وَ + genitive noun pattern as most distinctive Juz 'Amma feature
- Show 3-4 examples from Ash-Shams, Al-Layl, Ad-Duha, At-Tin
- GrammarTable from 16-RESEARCH.md showing oath patterns (surah, structure, genitive marker, number of oaths, response, verse location)
- Key grammatical rule: Oath وَ ALWAYS takes genitive case (distinguishes from conjunction وَ)
- Analysis: Variable oath count (2-10), consistent genitive case, response uses إِنَّ or لَقَدْ or negation مَا

**Section 3 — Pattern 2: Short Verse Rhythmic Structures:**
- Verse-ending patterns (fawasil) creating rhythmic prose (saj')
- Examples: -ān/-īm endings (Al-Fatiha), -ā endings (Ad-Duha), -ūn endings (Al-Kafirun)
- How grammatical forms are chosen to maintain rhythm while preserving meaning
- Show how case markers and pronoun suffixes contribute to consistent endings

**Section 4 — Pattern 3: Eschatological Vocabulary Patterns:**
- إِذَا + past tense: temporal structure for Day of Judgment events
- Examples from At-Takwir (81:1-6): "إِذَا ٱلشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ" series
- Passive verb dominance in eschatological passages
- Repeated structure: temporal clause + passive verb = cosmic upheaval

**Callout type="warning" (from 16-RESEARCH.md Pitfall 4 — Juz Amma over-generalization):**
"These patterns are COMMON in Juz 'Amma's short Makkan surahs. Longer Madinan surahs (Al-Baqarah, Al-Imran) use additional and different structures. Learn pattern recognition as a SKILL, not as 'all Quran follows these patterns.'"

  3. Rhythm analysis (intermediate): "Compare the verse endings in Surah Ad-Duha (93) with Al-Layl (92). What rhyme patterns do you notice? How do grammatical forms contribute?"
  4. Independent pattern (advanced): "Choose any 3 surahs from Juz 'Amma. Apply the 4-step pattern method to identify ONE grammatical pattern they share. Catalog examples, analyze function, compare variations."

- Learning Objectives:

- Connection: Reference L5.06 (pattern method applied to a different genre). Prerequisites: L5.01 (method), L4.03 (conditionals in du'a).

**Section 2 — Du'a Pattern 1: Imperative + Attached Pronoun:**
- Formula: Imperative verb + نَا (first-person plural pronoun)
- Example: ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَاطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ [Al-Fatiha 1:6]
  - ٱهْدِ: imperative from defective verb ه-د-ي
  - نَا: attached object pronoun "us"
  - ٱلصِّرَاطَ: second object (double-transitive verb), accusative
- More examples from Al-Baqarah 2:201: رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي ٱلدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً
  - رَبَّنَا: vocative (munada), accusative, mudaf + attached pronoun
  - آتِنَا: imperative + نَا
- Cross-surah comparison table of imperative du'a patterns

**Section 3 — Du'a Pattern 2: Vocative Constructions (رَبَّنَا / رَبِّ):**
- رَبَّنَا (Our Lord) — accusative mudaf with attached pronoun, used in communal du'a
- رَبِّ (My Lord) — vocative with kasra, used in personal du'a (prophets)
- Examples from Ibrahim (14:35-41): Prophet Ibrahim's series of رَبِّ du'a
- Examples from Al-Baqarah (2:127-129): Ibrahim and Isma'il's رَبَّنَا du'a
- Grammar: vocative particle يَا deleted before رَبّ (common Quranic pattern)

**Section 4 — Du'a Pattern 3: Conditional + Request:**
- إِنْ/إِذَا + condition → du'a response
- Negative petition: لَا + subjunctive (رَبَّنَا لَا تُؤَاخِذْنَا — "Our Lord, do not hold us accountable")
- Examples from Al-Baqarah 2:286 and Al-Imran 3:8
- Grammar: لَا + jussive for prohibition/negative request patterns

**Section 5 — Cross-Surah Du'a Comparison:**
GrammarTable comparing du'a structures across surahs:

Show 5-6 examples comparing رَبَّنَا vs رَبِّ, imperative vs prohibition, simple vs conditional
