**Section 1 — Introduction:**
- Context: "Ayat al-Kursi is one of the most powerful verses in the Quran — a single verse containing nominal sentences, negation, conditionals, relative clauses, and multiple idafah chains. It's a masterclass in grammatical complexity."
- Learning Objectives:
  1. Apply the 5-step method to a complex single verse
  2. Identify how multiple grammar structures layer within one verse

**Section 2 — Verse Overview:**
- Full verse displayed with ArabicExample: "ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَيُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَهُ مَا فِي ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِي ٱلْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِۦ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَلِيُّ ٱلْعَظِيمُ" [Al-Baqarah 2:255]
- Segmentation: Break the verse into 9 grammatical clauses/statements
- GrammarTable showing each clause's type and key structure

**Section 3 — Clause-by-Clause Analysis:**
Apply the 5-step method to each clause. Full three-level analysis for key words in each clause.

**Clause 1: ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ** — "Allah, there is no god except Him"
- ٱللَّهُ: mubtada (1st sentence) OR topic, nominative
- لَآ إِلَٰهَ: لَا النافية للجنس (genus-negating la), إِلَٰهَ is its ism (accusative, fatha)
- إِلَّا: exception particle (istithna')
- هُوَ: separate pronoun, nominative (khabar of la or badal)
- Grammar synthesis: Uses exception structure from L4.05 — complete affirmative exception

**Clause 2: ٱلْحَيُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ** — Two divine names as na't/khabar
- Both nominative, Pattern analysis: فَعِيل and فَيْعُول forms
- Root ح-ي-ي and Root ق-و-م

**Clause 3: لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ** — Negation with لَا + present tense
- سِنَةٌ as fa'il (subject AFTER verb), وَلَا نَوْمٌ as 'atf (conjunction)
- Balagha: lesser (drowsiness) mentioned before greater (sleep) = gradation (tadarruj)

**Clause 4: لَهُ مَا فِي ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِي ٱلْأَرْضِ** — Possessive + relative
- لَهُ: khabar muqaddam (fronted predicate), jarr wa-majrur
- مَا: ism mawsul (relative pronoun), mubtada mu'akhkhar
- Idafah chain and balagha of taqdim

**Clauses 5-9:** Continue systematic analysis for remaining clauses:
- مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِۦ — Rhetorical question + exception
- يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ — Knowledge encompasses (spatial metaphor)
- وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ — Negation + exception + conditional will
- وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ — Verbal sentence, past tense metaphor
- وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا وَهُوَ ٱلْعَلِيُّ ٱلْعَظِيمُ — Final clause + closing attributes

Provide full i'rab for key words in each clause. Not every single word needs exhaustive analysis — focus on pedagogically significant structures (negation patterns, exception particles, relative clauses, idafah chains).

**Section 4 — Verse-Level Synthesis:**
- Pattern analysis: How the verse builds from identity (clause 1) → attributes (2-3) → ownership (4) → authority (5) → knowledge (6-7) → power (8-9)
- Grammar serving theology: Every grammatical structure serves a theological function
- GrammarTable showing the 9 clauses with their theological theme and key grammar structure

**Section 5 — Practice:**
- 4 ExerciseBox:
  1. Segmentation (guided): "Divide Ayat al-Kursi into its 9 grammatical clauses. For each, identify: Is it nominal or verbal?"
  2. Exception analysis (intermediate): "Find all three exception structures (إِلَّا) in the verse. For each, identify what is being excepted and why."
  3. Negation patterns (intermediate): "The verse uses لَا three times. Analyze each: What type of لَا is it? What does it negate? How does the grammar differ?"
  4. Synthesis (advanced): "Choose any ONE clause and perform complete 5-step analysis. Include nahw, sarf, and balagha."

**Section 6 — Related Lessons:** L5.01 (method), L5.02 (Al-Fatiha comparison), L4.05 (exceptions), L4.10 (negation), L2.07 (prepositions)
