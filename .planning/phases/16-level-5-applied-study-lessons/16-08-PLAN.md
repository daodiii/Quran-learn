**Section 2 — Interrogative Particles:**

GrammarTable: Arabic Interrogative Particles
| Particle | Arabic | Meaning | Grammatical Effect | Example |
| أَ (hamza) | أَ | Is...? (yes/no) | Precedes verb or noun | أَلَمْ تَرَ |
| هَلْ | هَلْ | Is...? (yes/no) | Precedes verb | هَلْ أَتَىٰ |
| مَا | مَا | What? | Interrogative pronoun | مَا هَٰذَا |
| مَن | مَن | Who? | Interrogative pronoun | مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِي |
| كَيْفَ | كَيْفَ | How? | Adverb of manner | كَيْفَ تَكْفُرُونَ |
| أَيّ | أَيّ | Which? | Interrogative adjective | بِأَيِّ آلَاءِ |
| أَنَّى | أَنَّى | From where? How? | Interrogative adverb | أَنَّىٰ يَكُونُ |

Key grammar note: أَ (hamza al-istifham) vs أَ (hamza in other functions) — position and context determine meaning.

**Section 3 — Rhetorical Question Types:**

**Type 1: Istifham Inkari (إِنْكَارِي) — Denial/Rebuke:**
- Question form implying negative answer: "Is it possible that...?" (No!)
- Example: أَفَأَمِنتُم أَن يَخْسِفَ بِكُمْ [Al-Isra 17:68] — "Do you feel safe that He won't cause the earth to swallow you?" = You should NOT feel safe
- Grammar: أَ + past tense → denial of the action's appropriateness

**Type 2: Istifham Taqriri (تَقْرِيرِي) — Affirmation:**
- Question form implying positive answer: "Isn't it true that...?" (Yes!)
- Example: أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ [Ash-Sharh 94:1] — "Did We not expand your chest?" = We certainly DID
- Grammar: أَلَمْ + jussive → double negation (question + negation) = strong affirmation

**Type 3: Istifham Ta'ajjubi (تَعَجُّبِي) — Astonishment:**
- Question expressing wonder/amazement
- Example: كَيْفَ تَكْفُرُونَ بِٱللَّهِ [Al-Baqarah 2:28] — "How can you deny Allah?" = Astonishing denial
- Grammar: كَيْفَ (how) in context where "how" = "how can you possibly"

**Section 2 — Standard Arabic Word Order:**

**Verbal Sentences:** VSO (Verb + Subject + Object)
- Standard: عَبَدَ ٱلْمُسْلِمُونَ ٱللَّهَ (the Muslims worshiped Allah)
- Order: Verb first, then subject (fa'il), then object (maf'ul bihi)

**Nominal Sentences:** Mubtada + Khabar
- Standard: ٱللَّهُ رَحِيمٌ (Allah is merciful)
- Order: Subject (mubtada) first, then predicate (khabar)

**Section 3 — Taqdim Types and Their Effects:**
Follow the 16-RESEARCH.md Pattern 5 Applied Balagha structure.

**Type 1: Object Fronting (Taqdim al-Maf'ul) — Exclusivity:**
- إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ [Al-Fatiha 1:5]
- Standard: نَعْبُدُكَ → Quranic: إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ
- Effect: Exclusivity (qasr) — "You ALONE we worship"
- Full analysis following 16-RESEARCH.md comparative structure

**Type 2: Predicate Fronting (Taqdim al-Khabar) — Ownership/Priority:**
- لِلَّهِ ٱلْمَشْرِقُ وَٱلْمَغْرِبُ [Al-Baqarah 2:142]
- Standard: ٱلْمَشْرِقُ وَٱلْمَغْرِبُ لِلَّهِ → Quranic: لِلَّهِ ٱلْمَشْرِقُ
- Effect: Allah's ownership emphasized before what is owned

**Type 3: Adverbial Fronting (Taqdim al-Zarf/Jarr wa-Majrur):**
- فِي قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌ [Al-Baqarah 2:10]
- Standard: مَرَضٌ فِي قُلُوبِهِم → Quranic: فِي قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌ
- Effect: Location (hearts) emphasized before disease — focuses on WHERE the problem resides

**Type 4: Subject Delay (Ta'khir al-Fa'il):**
- يَخْشَى ٱللَّهَ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ ٱلْعُلَمَاءُ [Fatir 35:28]
- Standard: ٱلْعُلَمَاءُ يَخْشَوْنَ ٱللَّهَ → Quranic: يَخْشَى ٱللَّهَ ... ٱلْعُلَمَاءُ
- Effect: Allah (object of fear) fronted → emphasis on WHO is feared, not who fears
