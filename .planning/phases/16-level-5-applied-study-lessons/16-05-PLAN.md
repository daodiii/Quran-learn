**Section 2 — Oath Grammar Structure:**

**Three Oath Particles:**
1. **وَ (waw al-qasam)** — Most common in Quran, used exclusively with Allah's name or created things
2. **بِ (ba al-qasam)** — Used primarily with Allah: بِٱللَّهِ
3. **تَ (ta al-qasam)** — Rare, used with Allah: تَٱللَّهِ (as in Yusuf 12:73, 12:91)

**Grammatical Rule:** After ALL oath particles, the noun takes GENITIVE case (jarr). This is ALWAYS true — no exceptions.

**How to distinguish oath وَ from conjunction وَ:**
- Oath وَ: followed by genitive noun WITHOUT prior verb/noun to conjoin TO
- Conjunction وَ: connects two parallel items (noun+noun, verb+verb)
- Test: Does the وَ appear at the START of a sentence/passage? → Likely oath
- Test: Is the noun after وَ in genitive without an obvious governing preposition? → Likely oath

GrammarTable: Oath Particles
| Particle | Arabic | Frequency | Typical Usage | Case of Following Noun |
| Waw | وَ | ~100 times | Created things, oaths in Juz 'Amma | Always genitive |
| Ba | بِ | Less common | With Allah's name (بِٱللَّهِ) | Always genitive |
| Ta | تَ | Rare | With Allah's name (تَٱللَّهِ) | Always genitive |

**Section 3 — Oath Response (Jawab al-Qasam):**
- The response is what the oath emphasizes — the POINT of swearing
- Response markers: إِنَّ (indeed), لَقَدْ (certainly), لَ (emphatic lam), مَا (negation)
- Sometimes the response is understood/implied
