1. **Independent pronouns** - Stand-alone words (like "I", "you", "he" in English)
2. **Attached pronouns** - Suffixes attached to verbs, nouns, or particles (like possessive 's or object pronouns)
3. **Demonstrative pronouns** - Pointing words ("this", "that", "these", "those")
4. **Relative pronouns** - Connecting words ("who", "which", "that" in relative clauses)

**Section 1: Independent Pronouns (الضَّمَائِر المُنْفَصِلَة)**

Introduction paragraph explaining these are standalone pronoun words, used as subjects or after certain particles.

  caption="Independent Pronouns"

  rows={[
    ["1st (I/we)", "أَنَا (anā)", "—", "نَحْنُ (naḥnu)"],

    ["3rd masc. (he/they)", "هُوَ (huwa)", "هُمَا (humā)", "هُمْ (hum)"],
    ["3rd fem. (she/they)", "هِيَ (hiya)", "هُمَا (humā)", "هُنَّ (hunna)"]
  ]}
/>

**Usage notes:**
- Used as subjects in nominal sentences
- Used for emphasis (since verbs already indicate person)
- Can follow certain particles (إِنَّ، لَيْسَ)

**Quranic Example:**

<ArabicExample
  arabic="إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ"
  transliteration="iyyāka naʿbudu wa-iyyāka nastaʿīnu"
  translation="You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help"
  reference="Al-Fatiha 1:5"
  highlight="نَحْنُ"
/>

The pronoun نَحْنُ (we) is implied by the verb نَعْبُدُ but not stated (Arabic verbs contain their subjects).

**Section 3: Demonstrative Pronouns (أَسْمَاء الإِشَارَة)**

Introduction explaining these point to things/people (near vs. far).

<GrammarTable
  caption="Demonstrative Pronouns"
  headers={["Distance", "Singular Masc.", "Singular Fem.", "Dual", "Plural"]}
  rows={[
    ["Near", "هَٰذَا (hādhā)", "هَٰذِهِ (hādhihi)", "هَٰذَانِ / هَٰذَيْنِ", "هَٰؤُلَاءِ (hāʾulāʾi)"],
    ["Far", "ذَٰلِكَ (dhālika)", "تِلْكَ (tilka)", "ذَٰنِكَ / ذَيْنِكَ", "أُولَٰئِكَ (ulāʾika)"]
  ]}
/>

**Usage notes:**
- Demonstratives can function as subjects, objects, or adjectives
- When used as adjectives, they follow the noun
- Near vs. far distinction (هَٰذَا = this, ذَٰلِكَ = that)

**Quranic Example:**

<ArabicExample
  arabic="ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ"
  transliteration="dhālika l-kitābu lā rayba fīhi"
  translation="This is the Book about which there is no doubt"
  reference="Al-Baqarah 2:2"
  highlight="ذَٰلِكَ"
/>

ذَٰلِكَ (that/this) refers to the Quran being described.

**Learn more:**
- [L3.18: Demonstrative Pronouns](/learn/level-3/demonstrative-pronouns)

**Section 4: Relative Pronouns (الأَسْمَاء المَوْصُولَة)**

Introduction explaining these connect relative clauses (who, which, that in English).

<GrammarTable
  caption="Relative Pronouns"
  headers={["Gender/Number", "Pronoun", "Transliteration", "Meaning"]}
  rows={[
    ["Masc. Singular", "الَّذِي", "alladhī", "who/which/that (m.s.)"],
    ["Fem. Singular", "الَّتِي", "allatī", "who/which/that (f.s.)"],
    ["Dual", "اللَّذَانِ / اللَّذَيْنِ", "alladhāni / alladhayni", "who/which (dual)"],
    ["Masc. Plural", "الَّذِينَ", "alladhīna", "who/which/that (m.pl.)"],
    ["Fem. Plural", "اللَّاتِي / اللَّوَاتِي", "allātī / allawātī", "who/which/that (f.pl.)"],
    ["Common (all)", "مَن", "man", "who (for people)"],
    ["Common (all)", "مَا", "mā", "what/that which (for things)"]
  ]}
/>

**Usage notes:**
- الَّذِي and its variants agree with antecedent in gender/number
- مَن used for people regardless of gender/number
- مَا used for non-human things or abstract concepts

**Quranic Example:**

<ArabicExample
  arabic="الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ"
  transliteration="al-ḥamdu li-llāhi lladhī khalaqa s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍa"
  translation="All praise is for Allah who created the heavens and the earth"
  reference="Al-An'am 6:1"
  highlight="الَّذِي"
/>

الَّذِي (who) introduces a relative clause describing Allah.

- [L4.08: Relative Clauses](/learn/level-4/relative-clauses)
