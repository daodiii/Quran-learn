# Verb Conjugation Tables

Master the complete conjugation system for all ten Arabic verb forms. These tables show every possible combination of person (1st, 2nd, 3rd), number (singular, dual, plural), gender (masculine, feminine), and tense (past, present, imperative) for each verb pattern.

## How to Use These Tables

Arabic verbs follow predictable patterns based on their **form** (وَزْن / wazn). Each form has a distinct morphological pattern that conveys specific meanings:

- **Forms I-III**: Basic actions and their variations
- **Forms IV-VI**: Causative and reflexive meanings
- **Forms VII-X**: Advanced passive, reflexive, and seeking patterns

The tables use **root letters** (ك ت ب for writing, ف ع ل as the abstract template) to demonstrate each pattern. Once you memorize the pattern for a form, you can conjugate any root that follows it.

<Callout type="tip" title="Learning Strategy">
Focus on mastering Forms I, II, IV, V, and X first—these cover 90% of Quranic verbs. Forms III, VI, VII, VIII, and IX appear less frequently but follow the same systematic patterns.
</Callout>

<h2 id="form-i">Form I (فَعَلَ)</h2>

**Pattern:** فَعَلَ (faʿala)
**Meaning:** Basic action, the foundation of all verb forms
**Example Root:** ك ت ب (k-t-b) meaning "to write"

Form I is the simplest and most common verb pattern in Arabic. It represents the basic, unadorned meaning of the root. From this foundational form, all other forms (II-X) are derived with additional patterns that modify the meaning.

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form I Vowel Variations">
Form I verbs have three possible middle vowel patterns in the present tense:

- **يَفْعُلُ** (a-u pattern): كَتَبَ ← يَكْتُبُ (to write)
- **يَفْعِلُ** (a-i pattern): فَتَحَ ← يَفْتَحُ (to open)
- **يَفْعَلُ** (a-a pattern): ذَهَبَ ← يَذْهَبُ (to go)

These patterns must be memorized for each verb, but dictionaries always indicate which pattern a verb follows.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>**Weak Verb Variations** (click to expand)</summary>

Form I weak verbs (with و, ي, or ا in the root) follow modified patterns:

**Hollow Verbs (middle weak):** قَالَ (qāla - to say)
- Past: قَالَ، قَالَتْ، قَالُوا، قُلْتُ
- Present: يَقُولُ، تَقُولُ، يَقُولُونَ، أَقُولُ

**Defective Verbs (final weak):** رَمَى (ramā - to throw)
- Past: رَمَى، رَمَتْ، رَمَوْا، رَمَيْتُ
- Present: يَرْمِي، تَرْمِي، يَرْمُونَ، أَرْمِي

**Assimilated Verbs (initial weak):** وَجَدَ (wajada - to find)
- Follows regular Form I pattern mostly
- Imperative drops initial و: جِدْ (jid - find!)

</details>

<h2 id="form-ii">Form II (فَعَّلَ)</h2>

**Pattern:** فَعَّلَ (faʿʿala) - *doubled middle radical*
**Meaning:** Intensive, causative, or making something happen
**Example Root:** ع ل م (ʿ-l-m) meaning "knowledge/to know"

Form II takes the basic action of Form I and intensifies it or makes it causative. The key feature is **doubling the middle root letter** (indicated by شَدَّة / shadda). Example: كَسَرَ (to break) → كَسَّرَ (to shatter into pieces).

### Quranic Example

<ArabicExample
  arabic="وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ الْأَسْمَاءَ كُلَّهَا"
  transliteration="wa-ʿallama ādama l-asmāʾa kullahā"
  translation="And He taught Adam all the names"

  highlight="عَلَّمَ"
/>

The verb **عَلَّمَ** (ʿallama) is Form II, meaning "to teach" (causative of عَلِمَ "to know"). Allah caused Adam to know/learn the names—this is the intensive/causative force of Form II.

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form II Meaning">
Form II consistently means one of three things:

1. **Intensive action:** كَسَرَ (to break) → كَسَّرَ (to shatter/break repeatedly)
2. **Causative:** عَلِمَ (to know) → عَلَّمَ (to cause to know = to teach)
3. **Declaration:** كَذَبَ (to lie) → كَذَّبَ (to declare someone a liar)

Notice the doubled middle radical in all cases: **فَعَّلَ**
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>**Weak Verb Variations** (click to expand)</summary>

Form II weak verbs maintain the doubled middle radical but show modifications:

**Hollow Verbs (middle weak):** قَوَّمَ (qawwama - to straighten/establish)
- Past: قَوَّمَ، قَوَّمَتْ، قَوَّمُوا
- Present: يُقَوِّمُ، تُقَوِّمُ، يُقَوِّمُونَ

**Defective Verbs (final weak):** زَكَّى (zakkā - to purify)
- Past: زَكَّى، زَكَّتْ، زَكَّوْا، زَكَّيْتُ
- Present: يُزَكِّي، تُزَكِّي، يُزَكُّونَ، أُزَكِّي

<h2 id="form-iii">Form III (فَاعَلَ)</h2>

**Pattern:** فَاعَلَ (fāʿala) - *long ā after first radical*
**Meaning:** Attempting, mutual action, or doing something with/to someone
**Example Root:** ج ه د (j-h-d) meaning "effort/struggle"

Form III inserts a long **alif** (ا) between the first and second root letters. This typically expresses attempting to do something or doing it with someone else (mutual action). Example: قَتَلَ (to kill) → قَاتَلَ (to fight/battle with).

3. **Doing toward someone:** قَاتَلَ (to fight against), جَاهَدَ (to strive)

The long **ā** (alif) is the distinguishing feature: **فَاعَلَ**

<h2 id="form-iv">Form IV (أَفْعَلَ)</h2>

**Pattern:** أَفْعَلَ (afʿala) - *hamza prefix*
**Meaning:** Causative (making someone do the action)
**Example Root:** ن ز ل (n-z-l) meaning "to descend"

Form IV adds a **hamza** (أ) prefix and often means causing the basic action to happen. Example: نَزَلَ (to descend) → أَنْزَلَ (to send down/cause to descend). This is the most common causative form in the Quran.

### Past Tense (الْمَاضِي / al-māḍī)

<VerbConjugation
  root="ن ز ل"

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form IV vs Form II">
Both Forms II and IV can be causative, but:

- **Form II (فَعَّلَ):** More intensive, often transitive from intransitive. Example: فَرِحَ (to be happy) → فَرَّحَ (to make happy/gladden)

Form IV is more frequent with divine actions (أَنْزَلَ, أَعْطَى, أَحْيَا).

<h2 id="form-v">Form V (تَفَعَّلَ)</h2>

**Pattern:** تَفَعَّلَ (tafaʿʿala) - *ta- prefix + doubled middle radical*

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form V Reflexive Meaning">
Form V is systematically the reflexive of Form II:

- Form II: عَلَّمَ (to teach someone)
- Form V: تَعَلَّمَ (to teach oneself = to learn)

- Form II: طَهَّرَ (to purify something)
- Form V: تَطَهَّرَ (to purify oneself)

- Form II: كَلَّمَ (to speak to someone)
- Form V: تَكَلَّمَ (to speak/talk)

The **تَـ** prefix signals "to oneself" or "self-initiated action."
</Callout>

The verb **اِنْفَطَرَتْ** (infaṭarat) means "it split/broke open" (passive). The sky doesn't split itself actively—it undergoes splitting. This passive/intransitive meaning is typical of Form VII.

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form VII Usage">
Form VII expresses passive or reflexive meanings of Form I:

- **Passive sense:** كَسَرَ (to break) → اِنْكَسَرَ (to be broken, to break)
- **Reflexive sense:** صَرَفَ (to turn away) → اِنْصَرَفَ (to depart, go away)
- **Natural occurrence:** فَتَحَ (to open) → اِنْفَتَحَ (to become opened, open up)

Form VII is **less common** in the Quran than Forms I-VI, but appears in vivid descriptions of natural phenomena.
</Callout>

The verb **اِسْتَغْفِرُوا** (istaghfirū) is Form X imperative plural ("seek forgiveness!"). It means actively requesting Allah's forgiveness, not just acknowledging it.

### Pattern Notes

<Callout type="rule" title="Form X Primary Meanings">
Form X typically expresses one of three meanings:

1. **Seeking/requesting:** اِسْتَغْفَرَ (to seek forgiveness), اِسْتَعَانَ (to seek help)
2. **Considering something to be:** اِسْتَحْسَنَ (to consider good), اِسْتَكْبَرَ (to consider oneself great = to be arrogant)
3. **Finding something to be:** اِسْتَصْعَبَ (to find difficult)

**Recognition:** The prefix **اِسْتَـ** is always the marker of Form X.
</Callout>

<details>
<summary>**Weak Verb Variations** (click to expand)</summary>

Form X weak verbs maintain the اِسْتَـ prefix:

**Defective Verbs (final weak):** اِسْتَوَى (istawā - to settle, be established)
- Past: اِسْتَوَى، اِسْتَوَتْ، اِسْتَوَوْا، اِسْتَوَيْتُ
- Present: يَسْتَوِي، تَسْتَوِي، يَسْتَوُونَ، أَسْتَوِي

**Hollow Verbs:** اِسْتَقَامَ (istaqāma - to be upright/straight)
- Past: اِسْتَقَامَ، اِسْتَقَامَتْ، اِسْتَقَامُوا، اِسْتَقَمْتُ
- Present: يَسْتَقِيمُ، تَسْتَقِيمُ، يَسْتَقِيمُونَ، أَسْتَقِيمُ

**Assimilated Verbs:** اِسْتَوْصَى (istawṣā - to enjoin, recommend)
- Follows regular pattern despite initial و

</details>

## Summary of All Ten Forms

<Callout type="tip" title="Quick Reference Chart">

| Form | Pattern | Key Feature | Typical Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| I | فَعَلَ | Basic root | Simple action | كَتَبَ (wrote) |
| II | فَعَّلَ | Doubled middle | Intensive/causative | عَلَّمَ (taught) |
| III | فَاعَلَ | Long ā | Mutual/attempting | جَاهَدَ (strove) |
| IV | أَفْعَلَ | Hamza prefix | Causative | أَنْزَلَ (sent down) |
| V | تَفَعَّلَ | تَـ + doubled | Reflexive of II | تَعَلَّمَ (learned) |
| VI | تَفَاعَلَ | تَـ + long ā | Reflexive of III | تَبَارَكَ (blessed) |
| VII | اِنْفَعَلَ | اِنْـ prefix | Passive/reflexive | اِنْفَطَرَ (split) |
| VIII | اِفْتَعَلَ | ت inserted | Reflexive/intensive | اِسْتَمَعَ (listened) |
| IX | اِفْعَلَّ | Doubled final | Colors/defects | اِحْمَرَّ (became red) |
| X | اِسْتَفْعَلَ | اِسْتَـ prefix | Seeking/requesting | اِسْتَغْفَرَ (sought forgiveness) |

</Callout>

3. **Apply the meaning pattern** (causative, reflexive, seeking, etc.)
4. **Check the person/number/gender** from the conjugation endings
