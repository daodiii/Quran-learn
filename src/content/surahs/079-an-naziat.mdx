# An-Naziʿat (Those Who Drag Forth)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 46
- **Theme:** The Day of Resurrection and the story of Pharaoh's defiance - warnings about the certainty of judgment and the fate of tyrants who reject divine authority.
- **Grammar Focus:** Oath constructions with active participles, narrative past tense sequences, interrogative-rhetorical structures, conditional particles (idha), temporal adverbs (yawma'idhin), and complex embedded clauses.

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ غَرْقًا

**Translation:** "By those [angels] who extract violently"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ | wa-n-naazi'aati | ن ز ع | Particle + Noun - feminine plural, definite, active participle | Oath particle wa + Object of oath (muqsam bihi) - genitive (majrur) | By those who extract |
| 2 | غَرْقًا | gharqan | غ ر ق | Noun - verbal noun (masdar), indefinite, accusative | Adverbial accusative (maf'ul mutlaq) describing manner | Violently/drowning |

#### Grammar Notes
- Opening oath formula (qasam) with wa- introducing the active participle used as a substantive
- The participle ٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ is feminine plural, describing angels
- غَرْقًا functions as a cognate accusative (maf'ul mutlaq) intensifying the action

|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

## Grammar Summary

**Major Grammatical Constructs:**

1. **Oath Sequences (verses 1-5):** Five oath constructions using wa- and fa- with active participles (ٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ, ٱلنَّـٰشِطَـٰتِ, etc.) followed by cognate accusatives (غَرْقًا, نَشْطًا). Classical qasam structure.

2. **Narrative Past Tense (verses 15-26):** The story of Moses and Pharaoh uses sequential past tense verbs with fa- connectives: نَادَىٰهُ, أَرَىٰهُ, كَذَّبَ, عَصَىٰ, أَدْبَرَ, etc. Demonstrates Arabic narrative prose.

3. **Conditional Constructions:**
   - إِذَا (verses 6, 34) for certain future conditions (Day of Judgment)
   - إِذْ (verse 16) for definite past time
   - أَمَّا...فَإِنَّ (verses 37-41) for categorical conditional (if A then B, if C then D)

4. **Interrogative-Rhetorical Structures:**
   - هَلْ (verses 15, 18) introducing questions
   - أَءَ (verses 10, 11, 27) for incredulous questions
   - أَيَّانَ (verse 42) questioning time
   - فِيمَ (verse 43) rhetorical "in what [position]"

5. **Emphasis Particles:**
   - إِنَّ and its sisters (verses 26, 39, 41, 45) for emphatic nominal sentences
   - لَـ emphatic prefix (verses 10, 26)
   - إِنَّمَا (verses 13, 45) for restriction/limitation

6. **Active Participles as Substantives:** Throughout surah - ٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ, ٱلسَّـٰبِحَـٰتِ, ٱلْمُدَبِّرَٰتِ, ٱلرَّاجِفَة, ٱلطَّآمَّة - participles functioning as nouns

7. **Temporal Adverbs:** يَوْمَ (verses 6, 35, 42), يَوْمَئِذٍۢ (verse 8), بَعْدَ ذَٰلِكَ (verse 30) - establishing time contexts

8. **Fronted Objects for Emphasis:** وَٱلْأَرْضَ (verse 30), وَٱلْجِبَالَ (verse 32) - object placed before verb for rhetorical emphasis

9. **Form IV Causatives:** أَرَىٰهُ (showed him), أَغْطَشَ (darkened), أَخْرَجَ (brought forth), أَرْسَىٰهَا (anchored) - common in creation descriptions

10. **Idafa Constructions:** حَدِيثُ مُوسَىٰ, مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ, نَكَالَ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ وَٱلْأُولَىٰ - possessive/descriptive relationships

**Cross-references:** This surah exemplifies advanced oath constructions (see Grammar Lesson on Qasam), conditional sentences with إِذَا and أَمَّا, narrative tense sequencing, and rhetorical interrogatives.
