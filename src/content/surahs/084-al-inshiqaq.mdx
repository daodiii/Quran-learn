# Al-Inshiqaq (The Splitting Open)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 25
- **Theme:** The splitting of the sky on the Day of Judgment, followed by accountability and human response to revelation
- **Grammar Focus:** Idha conditional constructions, fa- result clauses, oath formulas (la uqsimu), vocative particles (ya ayyuha), amma conditional with fa-, interrogative negatives

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** إِذَا السَّمَاءُ انشَقَّتْ

**Translation:** "When the sky splits open"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِذَا | idha | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Not declinable (mabni), introduces protasis | when |
| 2 | السَّمَاءُ | as-sama'u | س م و | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | the sky |
| 3 | انشَقَّتْ | inshaqqat | ش ق ق | Verb - Form VII, past, 3rd person feminine singular | Predicate (khabar) | split open |

#### Grammar Notes
- Form VII infa'ala pattern (inshaqqat) indicates reflexive action - the sky splits itself
- Begins conditional sequence paralleling Surah 81

## Grammar Summary

- **Conditional constructions**: idha clauses (verses 1-5) with delayed apodosis (v.6), establishing eschatological context
- **Oath formulas**: fa-la uqsimu (v.16) and wa + genitive (vv.17-18), building to emphatic conclusion (v.19)
- **Amma conditionals**: fa-amma...fa- structure (vv.7-9, 10-12) creating parallel contrasts between righteous and wicked
- **Passive voice**: Extensive use (inshaqqat, muddat, utiya, quri'a, yuhasabu) emphasizing divine agency
- **Vocative structures**: ya ayyuha al-insan (v.6) - formal address to humanity
- **Emphasis particles**: inna...kana constructions (vv.13,15), la-tarkabunna energetic mood (v.19)
- **Circumstantial (hal) constructions**: masruran (vv.9,13) describing state
- **Masdar mu'awwal**: an lan yahura (v.14) - interpreted infinitive as object of zanna
- **Rhetorical questions**: fa-ma lahum la yu'minun (v.20) - challenging disbelief
- **Exception structures**: illa alladhina (v.25) - excepting believers from punishment
- **Ironic language**: bashshirhum bi-'adhabin (v.24) - "good news" of punishment
- **Inverted nominal sentences**: lahum ajrun (v.25) - predicate before subject for emphasis
