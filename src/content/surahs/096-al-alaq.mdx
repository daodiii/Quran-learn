# Al-'Alaq (The Clot)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan (first verses revealed to the Prophet)
- **Verses:** 19
- **Theme:** The first revelation emphasizing the importance of reading/reciting in the name of Allah, knowledge through the pen, and warning against human arrogance and transgression. Describes divine knowledge and human accountability.
- **Grammar Focus:** Imperative constructions (iqra'), relative clauses, kalla negation/emphasis particle, conditional la'in constructions, divine name formulas, active participles

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** ٱقْرَأْ بِٱسْمِ رَبِّكَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ

**Translation:** "Read in the name of your Lord who created"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | ٱقْرَأْ | iqra' | ق ر ء | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Imperative verb (amr) - mabni 'ala sukun | Read/Recite |
| 2 | بِ | bi | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni), governs genitive | in/with |
| 3 | ٱسْمِ | ismi | س م و | Noun - masculine, singular, definite (mudaf) | Object of preposition - genitive (majrur) | name |
| 4 | رَبِّكَ | rabbika | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + attached pronoun | Mudaf ilayh - genitive (majrur) | your Lord |

- First command in the Quran: iqra' (imperative of qara'a)
- Bi-ismi rabbika is prepositional phrase with idafa construction
- Alladhī introduces relative clause (silat al-mawsul) modifying rabbika
- Khalaqa verb has implied object (everything)

### Verse 6

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

**Translation:** "Because he sees himself self-sufficient"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

### Verse 19

**Arabic:** كَلَّا لَا تُطِعْهُ وَٱسْجُدْ وَٱقْتَرِب ۩

**Translation:** "No! Do not obey him, but prostrate and draw near [to Allah]"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | كَلَّا | kallā | - | Particle - deterrence/negation | Not declinable (mabni), negation/warning | No! |
| 2 | لَا | lā | - | Particle - negation | Not declinable (mabni), prohibitive la (nahiy) | Do not |
| 3 | تُطِعْهُ | tuti'hu | ط و ع | Verb - Form IV, present tense, 2nd person masculine singular, jussive + pronoun | Verb in jussive (majzum) by la nahiy, hu = object | you obey him |
| 4 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), connects commands | And |
| 5 | ٱسْجُدْ | usjud | س ج د | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Imperative verb (amr) - mabni 'ala sukun | prostrate |

- La nahiy (prohibitive la) makes verb jussive: do not obey

- Iqtarib (Form VIII) means to come close/approach (to Allah)
- Sajdah symbol ۩ indicates prostration verse

## Grammar Summary

### Main Grammatical Constructs

1. **Imperative constructions**: Iqra' (verses 1, 3), usjud, iqtarib (verse 19) - different forms of command

2. **Relative clauses**: Alladhī structures throughout (verses 1, 4, 9) connecting descriptive information

3. **Kallā particle**: Used three times (verses 6, 15, 19) for emphatic negation/deterrence

4. **Inna emphasis**: Verses 6, 8 use inna for strong assertion

5. **Conditional constructions**:
   - Simple in (verses 11, 13)
   - Emphatic la'in... la- (verse 15)

6. **Jussive mood**:
   - After lam (verses 5, 14, 15)
   - After la nahiy (verse 19)
   - After lam amr (verse 17)

7. **Double object verbs**: 'Allama takes two objects (verse 5)

8. **Kāna constructions**: Verse 11 with prepositional phrase as khabar

9. **Active participles as adjectives**: Kādhibatin, khāti'atin (verse 16)

10. **Idafa (possessive) constructions**: Bi-ismi rabbika, nādiyahu, etc.

11. **Future constructions**:
    - Sa- for near future (verse 18)
    - La-sawfa implied in threats

12. **Rhetorical questions**: A-ra'ayta constructions (verses 9, 11, 13), a-lam ya'lam (verse 14)

13. **Badal (apposition)**: Verse 16 nāsiyatin as badal to an-nāsiyati

14. **Metonymy**: An-nāsiyah (forelock) representing the whole person

### Pedagogical Notes

This surah is excellent for teaching:
- The first revelation and its command structure
- Various forms of emphasis (inna, la-, kallā)
- Conditional sentence types (in vs. la'in)
- Jussive mood in multiple contexts (negation, prohibition, command)
- Rhetorical question patterns
- Contrast between divine knowledge and human arrogance
- Relative clause construction with alladhī
- The interplay between narrative and direct address
