# Al-Layl (The Night)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 21
- **Theme:** This surah begins with oaths by the night, day, and the creation of male and female. It teaches that human efforts and paths differ vastly: those who give, are pious, and believe in goodness will be eased toward righteousness, while the miserly who deny goodness will be eased toward hardship. It promises ultimate reward and punishment, and emphasizes God's warning and guidance.
- **Grammar Focus:** Oath constructions with وَ (wa), conditional particles أَمَّا (amma) with فَ (fa-), Form II and Form IV verbs, comparative structures, future tense verbs, negation particles

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَالَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَىٰ

**Translation:** "By the night when it covers"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - oath | Not declinable (mabni), oath particle | by |
| 2 | اللَّيْلِ | al-layli | ل-ي-ل | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Genitive (majrur) after oath particle وَ | the night |
| 3 | إِذَا | idha | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Not declinable (mabni), introduces when-clause | when |
| 4 | يَغْشَىٰ | yaghsha | غ-ش-ي | Verb - Form I, present, 3rd person masculine singular | Verb in indicative (marfu') | it covers |

#### Grammar Notes
- Opening oath by the night - وَ is oath particle taking genitive
- إِذَا introduces temporal clause: "when it covers [the day/earth]"
- يَغْشَىٰ present tense for recurring action, alif maqsura ending
- Object of يَغْشَىٰ is understood (the earth/day)

- الَّذِي begins relative clause describing الْأَتْقَى

**Arabic:** إِلَّا ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ الْأَعْلَىٰ

**Translation:** "But only seeking the countenance of his Lord, the Most High"

### Verse 21

**Arabic:** وَلَسَوْفَ يَرْضَىٰ

**Translation:** "And he is going to be satisfied"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), coordinates | and |
| 2 | لَ | la- | - | Particle - emphasis | Not declinable (mabni), emphasizes future verb | surely/certainly |
| 3 | سَوْفَ | sawfa | - | Particle - future | Not declinable (mabni), indicates future tense (more emphatic than سَ) | will/going to |
| 4 | يَرْضَىٰ | yarda | ر-ض-ي | Verb - Form I, present, 3rd person masculine singular | Verb in indicative (marfu') | he will be pleased/satisfied |

#### Grammar Notes
- وَلَسَوْفَ triple emphasis: وَ + لَ + سَوْفَ
- سَوْفَ stronger future particle than سَ (more distant/emphatic future)
- يَرْضَىٰ: ultimate satisfaction in Paradise
- Subject is الْأَتْقَى (the most righteous)
- Concludes surah with promise of divine reward and pleasure

## Grammar Summary

- **Oath constructions with وَ**: Verses 1-3 use وَ as oath particle taking genitive case
- **Temporal clauses with إِذَا**: Verses 1, 2, 11 use إِذَا to introduce when-clauses
- **إِنَّ emphasis structures**: Verses 4, 12, 13 use إِنَّ for emphasis, with لَ for double emphasis
- **أَمَّا...فَ conditional construction**: Verses 5-7 and 8-10 demonstrate "as for X, then Y" structure with perfect parallelism
- **Future tense particles**: سَ (verses 7, 10, 17) and سَوْفَ (verse 21) indicate future actions
- **Comparative/superlative structures**: الْيُسْرَىٰ vs. الْعُسْرَىٰ (ease vs. hardship), الْأَشْقَى vs. الْأَتْقَى (most wretched vs. most righteous), الْحُسْنَىٰ (the best), الْأَعْلَىٰ (the Most High)
- **Form II verbs (intensive/causative)**: صَدَّقَ, كَذَّبَ, نُيَسِّرُهُ, يُجَنَّبُهَا (passive)
- **Form IV verbs**: أَعْطَىٰ, يُغْنِي, أَنذَرْتُكُمْ, يُؤْتِي
- **Form V verbs (reflexive)**: تَجَلَّىٰ, تَرَدَّىٰ, تَوَلَّىٰ, تَلَظَّىٰ, يَتَزَكَّىٰ
- **Form VIII verbs**: اتَّقَىٰ, ابْتِغَاءَ (verbal noun)
- **Form X verb**: اسْتَغْنَىٰ (considering oneself to possess a quality)
- **Passive voice constructions**: يُجَنَّبُهَا, تُجْزَىٰ
- **Negation particles**: لَا (verses 11, 15), مَا (verses 11, 19)
- **Exception/restriction**: إِلَّا (verses 15, 20) for exception constructions
- **Perfect structural parallelism**: The surah exhibits beautiful symmetry between the two types of people (verses 5-7 vs. 8-10, verse 15 vs. 17)
- **Idafa constructions**: وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ, سَعْيَكُمْ
- **Emphasis patterns**: وَلَسَوْفَ (verse 21) combines three emphatic particles
