# Al-Ghashiyah (The Overwhelming)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 26
- **Theme:** The overwhelming terror of Judgment Day, contrasting fates of believers and disbelievers, signs in creation pointing to the Creator
- **Grammar Focus:** Interrogative hal, nominal descriptive sentences with adjective agreement, active participles as substantives, a-fa-la interrogative-negative constructions, commands to reflect on creation

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** هَلْ أَتَاكَ حَدِيثُ الْغَاشِيَةِ

**Translation:** "Has the story of the Overwhelming Event reached you?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | هَلْ | hal | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), introduces question | has/did |
| 2 | أَتَاكَ | ataka | أ ت ي | Verb - Form I, past, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Main verb + object pronoun | it came to you |
| 3 | حَدِيثُ | hadithu | ح د ث | Noun - masculine, singular, definite (mudaf) | Subject (fa'il) - nominative (marfu') | story/account |
| 4 | الْغَاشِيَةِ | al-ghashiyati | غ ش ي | Noun - active participle, feminine, singular, definite | Mudaf ilayhi - genitive (majrur) | the Overwhelming |

#### Grammar Notes
- hal ataka: rhetorical question drawing attention
- al-ghashiyah: active participle from ghashiya (to overwhelm/cover) - the Day that overwhelms all
- Characteristic Quranic opening creating suspense

## Grammar Summary

- **Interrogative hal**: Rhetorical question opening (v.1) drawing attention and creating suspense
- **Nominal descriptive sentences**: Extensive use with multiple predicates describing faces (vv.2-7, 8-16) - wujuhun khashi'atun 'amilatun nasibatun
- **Active participles as substantives**: Almost every adjective is an active/passive participle showing continuous state (khashi'ah, 'amilah, nasibah, hamiyah, na'imah, radiyah, etc.)
- **Inverted nominal sentences**: Predicate before subject for emphasis (vv.12-16: fiha 'aynun jariyah, etc.; vv.25-26: inna ilayna iyabahum)
- **Passive voice constructions**: Divine agency emphasized (khuliqat, rufi'at, nusibat, sutihat in vv.17-20)
- **Parallel structures**: Contrasting descriptions of two groups (vv.2-7 vs. 8-16); four creation signs (vv.17-20)
- **A-fa-la interrogative-negative**: Rhetorical questioning (v.17: a-fa-la yanzurun) - "Then do they not...?"
- **Ellipsis**: Repeated verb omitted in parallel constructions (vv.18-20: wa-ila as-sama'i implied yanzurun)
- **Restriction particles**: innama (v.21) confining meaning - "only a reminder"
- **Emphatic negation**: laysa + bi construction (v.22: lasta 'alayhim bi-musaytirin)
- **Inna with inverted structure**: Verses 25-26 use inna with predicate before subject for rhetorical emphasis
- **Sequential particles**: thumma (v.26) indicating sequence - first return, then reckoning
- **Form II intensive verbs**: yu'adhdhib (v.24), dhakkir (v.21) - intensified action
- **Absolute object (maf'ul mutlaq)**: al-'adhaba al-akbara (v.24) - emphasizing the punishment itself
