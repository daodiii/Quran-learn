# An-Naba (The Tidings)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 40
- **Theme:** Opens Juz Amma with questions about the Day of Judgment, followed by descriptions of Allah's creative power, contrasting rewards for the righteous with punishment for the deniers.
- **Grammar Focus:** Interrogative constructions ('amma), negative rhetorical questions (a-lam), ja'ala + double accusative, inna + accusative emphasis, temporal expressions (yawma), contrast structures, active participles

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** عَمَّ يَتَسَاءَلُونَ

**Translation:** "About what are they asking one another?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | عَمَّ | 'amma | ع-ن-ي | Particle - 'an + ma (interrogative) | Preposition + interrogative pronoun - majrur (genitive) location | about what |
| 2 | يَتَسَاءَلُونَ | yatasaa'aluun | س-أ-ل | Verb - Form VI, present, 3rd person masculine plural | Verb - indicative (marfu'), plural subject pronoun attached | they ask one another |

#### Grammar Notes
- 'Amma = 'an + ma: the preposition 'an contracts with the interrogative ma
- Form VI (tafa''ala) indicates mutual/reciprocal action: asking each other
- Opening interrogative sets the dramatic tone for the entire surah

**Translation:** "About the Great News"

**Arabic:** إِنَّا أَنذَرْنَاكُمْ عَذَابًا قَرِيبًا يَوْمَ يَنظُرُ الْمَرْءُ مَا قَدَّمَتْ يَدَاهُ وَيَقُولُ الْكَافِرُ يَا لَيْتَنِي كُنتُ تُرَابًا

**Translation:** "Indeed, We have warned you of a near punishment on the Day when a man will observe what his hands have put forth and the disbeliever will say, 'Oh, I wish that I were dust!'"

#### Grammar Notes
- Innaa andharnaakum: emphatic warning structure
- 'Adhaaban qariiban: near punishment (Day of Judgment is imminent)
- Yawma yanzhuru al-mar'u: each person observes their deeds
- Yadaahu: dual, metonymy for all one's actions
- Yaa laytanii kuntu turaaban: regretful wish of the disbeliever
- Layta: particle expressing impossible wish
- Final dramatic ending: the ultimate regret of deniers

## Grammar Summary

- **Interrogative constructions:** 'Amma ('an + ma) opening, a-lam rhetorical questions (vv.6-16)
- **Ja'ala structures:** Double accusative constructions (object + predicate complement) throughout creation descriptions
- **Inna emphasis:** Multiple inna + accusative constructions for emphasis (vv.17, 21, 31, 40)
- **Passive voice:** Extensive use in Day of Judgment descriptions (yunfakhu, futihat, suyyirat)
- **Active participles:** Used as nouns (al-taaghiina, al-muttaqiina, al-kaafiru) and adjectives (laabithiina)
- **Negative constructions:** Laa + present tense negation, lan + subjunctive for emphatic future negation
- **Exception structures:** Illaa with various grammatical roles
- **Temporal constructions:** Yawma + verbal clause defining the Day of Judgment
- **Contrast structures:** Hell section (vv.21-30) vs. Paradise section (vv.31-36) with parallel grammatical patterns
- **Idafa chains:** Rabbi al-samaawaati wa-al-ardhi wa-maa baynahumaa
- **Conditional structures:** Man shaa'a (whoever wills) with consequence
- **Wish constructions:** Layta + pronoun expressing impossible wish (v.40)
- **Haal (circumstantial qualifiers):** Azwaajan (v.8), saffan (v.38), describing states and manners
- **Maf'ul mutlaq (absolute objects):** Kidhdhaaban (v.28), jazaa'an (vv.26, 36) for emphasis
