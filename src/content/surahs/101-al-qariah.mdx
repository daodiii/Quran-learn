# Al-Qari'ah (The Striking Calamity)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 11
- **Theme:** Vivid depiction of the Day of Judgment (al-Qari'ah - the Striking Hour) when humanity will be scattered like moths and mountains crumbled like wool. Describes the fate of those whose scales are heavy (Paradise) versus light (Hell). Uses dramatic imagery and rhetorical questions.
- **Grammar Focus:** Interrogative ma constructions, exclamatory sentences, nominal sentences, similes with ka-, relative pronouns, conditional fa- constructions, idafa structures

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** ٱلْقَارِعَةُ

**Translation:** "The Striking Calamity"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | ٱلْقَارِعَةُ | al-qāri'atu | ق ر ع | Active participle - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (mubtada') without khabar (for emphasis) - nominative (marfu') | The Striking Calamity |

#### Grammar Notes
- Al-qāri'ah is active participle (ism fa'il) from qara'a (to strike/knock)
- Mubtada' without explicit khabar creates dramatic emphasis
- Refers to Day of Judgment with emphasis on its sudden striking nature
- One-word sentence creates powerful opening

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** مَا ٱلْقَارِعَةُ

**Translation:** "What is the Striking Calamity?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | مَا | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), question particle | What? |
| 2 | ٱلْقَارِعَةُ | al-qāri'atu | ق ر ع | Active participle - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | the Striking Calamity |

#### Grammar Notes
- Ma interrogative for rhetorical question
- Nominal sentence without explicit khabar (khabar is the question itself)
- Rhetorical device: asking what it is magnifies its importance
- Creates suspense before explanation

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْقَارِعَةُ

**Translation:** "And what can make you know what the Striking Calamity is?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | مَآ | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), question particle | What? |
| 3 | أَدْرَىٰكَ | adrāka | د ر ي | Verb - Form IV, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular + attached pronoun | Verb - mabni 'ala fatha maqdurah, ka = direct object | made you know |
| 4 | مَا | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), introduces second object clause | what |
| 5 | ٱلْقَارِعَةُ | al-qāri'atu | ق ر ع | Active participle - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | the Striking Calamity |

#### Grammar Notes
- Standard Quranic formula: wa-mā adrāka mā...
- Adrā (Form IV of darā) means to cause to know/comprehend
- First mā is interrogative, second mā introduces object clause
- Heightens mystery and grandeur of the event
- Implies: its reality is beyond human comprehension

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** يَوْمَ يَكُونُ ٱلنَّاسُ كَٱلْفَرَاشِ ٱلْمَبْثُوثِ

**Translation:** "The Day when people will be like scattered moths"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | يَوْمَ | yawma | ي و م | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite (mudaf) | Adverbial (zarf zaman or badal) - accusative (mansub) | Day/The Day |
| 2 | يَكُونُ | yakūnu | ك و ن | Verb - Form I, present tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Verb (describes the Day) - indicative (marfu') | will be |
| 3 | ٱلنَّاسُ | an-nāsu | ن و س | Noun - masculine, plural, definite | Ism kana (subject of yakūnu) - nominative (marfu') | the people |
| 4 | كَ | ka | - | Particle - preposition (comparison) | Not declinable (mabni), introduces simile | like |
| 5 | ٱلْفَرَاشِ | al-farāshi | ف ر ش | Noun - masculine, collective, definite | Object of preposition - genitive (majrur) | moths |
| 6 | ٱلْمَبْثُوثِ | al-mabthūthi | ب ث ث | Passive participle - masculine, singular, definite | Adjective modifying al-farāsh - genitive (majrur) | the scattered |

#### Grammar Notes
- Yawma can be badal (appositive) to al-qāri'ah or adverbial
- Yakūnu an-nāsu: kana sentence
- Ka- introduces simile (comparison particle)
- Al-mabthūth is passive participle (ism maf'ul) from baththa (to scatter)
- Image: people scattered in confusion like moths around light

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** وَتَكُونُ ٱلْجِبَالُ كَٱلْعِهْنِ ٱلْمَنفُوشِ

**Translation:** "And the mountains will be like carded wool"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | تَكُونُ | takūnu | ك و ن | Verb - Form I, present tense, 3rd person feminine singular | Verb coordinated with yakūnu - indicative (marfu') | will be |
| 3 | ٱلْجِبَالُ | al-jibālu | ج ب ل | Noun - feminine, plural, definite | Ism kana (subject of takūnu) - nominative (marfu') | the mountains |
| 4 | كَ | ka | - | Particle - preposition (comparison) | Not declinable (mabni), introduces simile | like |
| 5 | ٱلْعِهْنِ | al-'ihni | ع ه ن | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Object of preposition - genitive (majrur) | wool |
| 6 | ٱلْمَنفُوشِ | al-manfūshi | ن ف ش | Passive participle - masculine, singular, definite | Adjective modifying al-'ihn - genitive (majrur) | the carded/fluffed |

#### Grammar Notes
- Parallel structure to verse 4 (yakūnu... takūnu...)
- Al-jibāl (mountains) treated as feminine plural
- Al-manfūsh from nafasha (to card/fluff wool)
- Vivid contrast: solid mountains become like loose, colorful wool
- Both similes emphasize dissolution and scattering

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** فَأَمَّا مَنثَقُلَتْ مَوَٰزِينُهُۥ

**Translation:** "Then as for one whose scales are heavy"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), introduces consequence | Then/So |
| 2 | أَمَّا | ammā | - | Particle - conditional | Not declinable (mabni), introduces conditional | As for |
| 3 | مَن | man | - | Relative pronoun | Not declinable (mabni), subject of discussion | whoever/one who |
| 4 |ثَقُلَتْ | thaqulat | ث ق ل | Verb - Form I, past tense, 3rd person feminine singular | Verb in relative clause - mabni 'ala fath | were heavy |
| 5 | مَوَٰزِينُهُۥ | mawāzīnuhu | و ز ن | Noun - feminine, plural, definite + attached pronoun | Subject (fa'il) - nominative (marfu') | his scales |

#### Grammar Notes
- Fa-ammā construction introduces conditional categorization
- Man is relative pronoun (subject of interest)
- Thaqulat is intransitive (became heavy)
- Mawāzīn is plural of mīzān (scale/balance)
- Refers to scales weighing good deeds on Day of Judgment

### Verse 7

**Arabic:** فَهُوَ فِى عِيشَةٍۢ رَّاضِيَةٍۢ

**Translation:** "Then he will be in a pleasant life"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), answer to ammā | Then |
| 2 | هُوَ | huwa | - | Pronoun - detached, 3rd person masculine singular | Not declinable (mabni), subject (mubtada') | he |
| 3 | فِى | fī | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni), indicates location | in |
| 4 | عِيشَةٍۢ | 'īshatin | ع ي ش | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite | Object of preposition - genitive (majrur) | a life |
| 5 | رَّاضِيَةٍۢ | rādiyatin | ر ض و | Active participle - feminine, singular, indefinite | Adjective modifying 'īshatin - genitive (majrur) | pleasant/satisfying |

#### Grammar Notes
- Fa- introduces jawab (answer) to ammā condition
- Nominal sentence: huwa fī 'īshatin rādiyah
- Prepositional phrase fī 'īshatin is khabar
- Rādiyah (active participle) can mean "pleasing" or "pleased/satisfying"
- Refers to Paradise

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** وَأَمَّا مَنْ خَفَّتْ مَوَٰزِينُهُۥ

**Translation:** "But as for one whose scales are light"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And/But |
| 2 | أَمَّا | ammā | - | Particle - conditional | Not declinable (mabni), introduces conditional | As for |

### Verse 9

**Arabic:** فَأُمُّهُۥ هَاوِيَةٌۭ

**Translation:** "Then his refuge will be the Abyss"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), answer to ammā | Then |
| 2 | أُمُّهُۥ | ummuhu | ء م م | Noun - feminine, singular, definite + attached pronoun | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | his mother/refuge |
| 3 | هَاوِيَةٌۭ | hāwiyatun | ه و ي | Active participle - feminine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfu') | the Abyss/falling place |

#### Grammar Notes
- Fa- introduces answer to second ammā condition
- Ummuhu literally "his mother" (metaphorically: his dwelling/refuge)
- Hāwiyah is active participle from hawā (to fall/plunge)
- Proper name for a level/pit of Hell
- Ironic: instead of pleasant life, his "home" is the Abyss

### Verse 10

**Arabic:** وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا هِىَۡ

**Translation:** "And what can make you know what it is?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | مَآ | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), question particle | What? |
| 3 | أَدْرَىٰكَ | adrāka | د ر ي | Verb - Form IV, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular + attached pronoun | Verb - mabni 'ala fatha maqdurah, ka = object | made you know |

### Verse 11

**Arabic:** نَارٌ حَامِيَةٌۢ

**Translation:** "A scorching Fire"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | نَارٌ | nārun | ن و ر | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar for implied mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | a Fire |
| 2 | حَامِيَةٌۢ | hāmiyatun | ح م ي | Active participle - feminine, singular, indefinite | Adjective modifying nārun - nominative (marfu') | scorching/intensely hot |

#### Grammar Notes
- Answer to the question in verse 10
- Khabar for implied mubtada' (hiya = it is)
- Nār (Fire) is common term for Hell
- Hāmiyah is active participle from hamiya (to be intensely hot)
- Indefinite (tanwin) for emphasis on intensity
- Climactic ending: simple but terrifying description

## Grammar Summary

### Main Grammatical Constructs

1. **Interrogative ma constructions**: Verses 2, 3, 10 using mā for rhetorical questions

2. **Exclamatory wa-mā adrāka formula**: Verses 3, 10 - standard Quranic formula for magnification

3. **Nominal sentences**:
   - Verse 1: mubtada' without khabar
   - Verse 7: huwa fī 'īshatin rādiyah
   - Verse 9: ummuhu hāwiyah
   - Verse 11: (implied hiya) nārun hāmiyah

4. **Similes with ka-**: Verses 4-5 comparing people to moths, mountains to wool

5. **Kāna sentences**: Verses 4-5 (yakūnu an-nāsu, takūnu al-jibālu)

6. **Ammā conditional construction**: Verses 6-7, 8-9 (fa-ammā... fa-)

7. **Relative pronouns**: Man (whoever) in verses 6, 8

8. **Passive participles**: Al-mabthūth, al-manfūsh describing scattered/carded state

9. **Active participles**:
   - Al-qāri'ah (the striker)
   - Rādiyah (pleasant/satisfying)
   - Hāwiyah (falling place/Abyss)
   - Hāmiyah (scorching)

10. **Idafa constructions**: Minimal - mainly possessive pronouns (mawāzīnuhu, ummuhu)

11. **Parallel structures**: Verses 4-5 (people/mountains), verses 6-9 (heavy scales/light scales)

12. **Emphasis through repetition**: Al-qāri'ah mentioned 3 times (verses 1, 2, 3)

13. **Dramatic progression**: Question → Description → Division → Outcomes

14. **Metaphorical language**: Umm (mother) for dwelling/refuge

### Pedagogical Notes

This surah is excellent for teaching:
- The wa-mā adrāka rhetorical formula (common in Quran)
- Interrogative particles (mā) for emphasis and magnification
- Simile construction with ka-
- The fa-ammā conditional pattern for categorization
- Nominal sentences without explicit khabar (dramatic effect)
- Active and passive participles as descriptive adjectives
- Parallel structures for contrast (believers vs. disbelievers)
- Metaphorical use of everyday terms (umm, 'īshah)
- How brevity creates impact (verses 1, 11)
- Progression from question to description to judgment
- The use of vivid imagery in eschatological passages

### Literary Features

- **Ring composition**: Opens and closes with descriptions (al-qāri'ah / nār hāmiyah)
- **Intensification**: Three mentions of al-qāri'ah with increasing detail
- **Symmetry**: Two equal conditional outcomes (verses 6-7, 8-9)
- **Vivid imagery**: Moths, wool, fire - concrete images for abstract concepts
- **Dramatic pacing**: Short verses create urgency and impact
