# Al-Kafirun (The Disbelievers)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 6
- **Theme:** A clear declaration of the distinction between the faith of believers and disbelievers, emphasizing religious tolerance and separation of practices.
- **Grammar Focus:** Negation with لَا (la), present tense verbs, attached pronouns, repetitive parallel structures, nominal sentences

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الْكَافِرُونَ

**Translation:** "Say: O disbelievers"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | قُلْ | qul | ق و ل | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Command verb (fi'l amr) - mabni | Say |
| 2 | يَا | yā | - | Particle - vocative | Vocative particle (harf nida') - mabni | O |
| 3 | أَيُّهَا | ayyuhā | - | Demonstrative - vocative attention word | Called upon (munāda) - mabni | [vocative attention particle] |
| 4 | الْكَافِرُونَ | al-kāfirūn | ك ف ر | Noun - masculine plural, definite, active participle | Vocative apposition (badal) - nominative (marfu') | The disbelievers |

#### Grammar Notes
- The imperative verb قُلْ is a standard opening for many surahs, commanding the Prophet to speak.
- يَا أَيُّهَا is the vocative construction used for calling definite groups.
- الْكَافِرُونَ is an active participle (ism al-fa'il) in the masculine plural form, serving as the addressee.

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** لَا أَعْبُدُ مَا تَعْبُدُونَ

**Translation:** "I do not worship what you worship"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | لَا | lā | - | Particle - negation | Negative particle (harf nafy) - mabni | Not |
| 2 | أَعْبُدُ | a'budu | ع ب د | Verb - Form I, present tense, 1st person singular | Present tense verb (fi'l mudāri') - indicative (marfu') | I worship |
| 3 | مَا | mā | - | Pronoun - relative/indefinite | Relative pronoun or object (mawsul or maf'ul bihi) - mabni | What |
| 4 | تَعْبُدُونَ | ta'budūn | ع ب د | Verb - Form I, present tense, 2nd person masculine plural | Present tense verb (fi'l mudāri') - indicative (marfu') | You (plural) worship |

#### Grammar Notes
- لَا is the present/future negation particle.
- The verb أَعْبُدُ is negated by لَا, expressing a categorical present and future refusal.
- مَا here functions as a relative pronoun meaning "that which" or "what," serving as the object of both verbs.
- This establishes the first element of the parallel structure that continues throughout the surah.

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** وَلَا أَنتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ

**Translation:** "Nor are you worshippers of what I worship"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَلَا | wa-lā | - | Particle - conjunction + negation | Coordinating conjunction + negative particle - mabni | And not / nor |
| 2 | أَنتُمْ | antum | - | Pronoun - detached, 2nd person masculine plural | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | You (plural) |
| 3 | عَابِدُونَ | 'ābidūn | ع ب د | Noun - masculine plural, indefinite, active participle | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfu') | Worshippers |
| 4 | مَا | mā | - | Pronoun - relative/indefinite | Relative pronoun - mabni | What |
| 5 | أَعْبُدُ | a'budu | ع ب د | Verb - Form I, present tense, 1st person singular | Present tense verb (fi'l mudāri') - indicative (marfu') | I worship |

#### Grammar Notes
- This is a nominal sentence (jumlah ismiyyah) with أَنتُمْ as the subject and عَابِدُونَ as the predicate.
- The active participle عَابِدُونَ denotes continuous action ("worshippers" / "those who worship").
- The conjunction وَ connects this verse to the previous one in the pattern of reciprocal negation.

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** وَلَا أَنَا عَابِدٌ مَّا عَبَدتُّمْ

**Translation:** "And I am not a worshipper of what you have worshipped"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَلَا | wa-lā | - | Particle - conjunction + negation | Coordinating conjunction + negative particle - mabni | And not / nor |
| 2 | أَنَا | anā | - | Pronoun - detached, 1st person singular | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | I |
| 3 | عَابِدٌ | 'ābid | ع ب د | Noun - masculine singular, indefinite, active participle | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfu') | A worshipper |
| 4 | مَّا | mā | - | Pronoun - relative/indefinite | Relative pronoun - mabni | What |
| 5 | عَبَدتُّمْ | 'abadtum | ع ب د | Verb - Form I, past tense, 2nd person masculine plural | Past tense verb (fi'l māḍī) - mabni | You (plural) worshipped |

#### Grammar Notes
- Another nominal sentence structure parallel to verse 3, but with 1st person pronoun.
- Note the shift to past tense عَبَدتُّمْ, indicating "what you have worshipped" historically.
- The active participle عَابِدٌ is singular and indefinite, contrasting with the plural form in verse 3.

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** وَلَا أَنتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ

**Translation:** "Nor are you worshippers of what I worship"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَلَا | wa-lā | - | Particle - conjunction + negation | Coordinating conjunction + negative particle - mabni | And not / nor |
| 2 | أَنتُمْ | antum | - | Pronoun - detached, 2nd person masculine plural | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | You (plural) |
| 3 | عَابِدُونَ | 'ābidūn | ع ب د | Noun - masculine plural, indefinite, active participle | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfu') | Worshippers |
| 4 | مَا | mā | - | Pronoun - relative/indefinite | Relative pronoun - mabni | What |
| 5 | أَعْبُدُ | a'budu | ع ب د | Verb - Form I, present tense, 1st person singular | Present tense verb (fi'l mudāri') - indicative (marfu') | I worship |

#### Grammar Notes
- This verse is an exact repetition of verse 3, reinforcing the message through emphasis.
- The repetition (takrār) is a rhetorical device emphasizing the permanent separation in worship.

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** لَكُمْ دِينُكُمْ وَلِيَ دِينِ

**Translation:** "To you is your religion, and to me is my religion"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | لَكُمْ | lakum | - | Particle - preposition + attached pronoun 2nd person plural | Preposition + pronoun acting as predicate (khabar muqaddam) - mabni | To you / for you |
| 2 | دِينُكُمْ | dīnukum | د ي ن | Noun - masculine singular + attached pronoun 2nd person plural | Subject (mubtada' mu'akhkhar) - nominative (marfu') | Your religion |
| 3 | وَلِيَ | wa-liya | - | Conjunction + preposition + attached pronoun 1st person singular | Coordinating conjunction + preposition + pronoun acting as predicate (khabar muqaddam) - mabni | And to me / for me |
| 4 | دِينِ | dīni | د ي ن | Noun - masculine singular + implied attached pronoun 1st person singular | Subject (mubtada' mu'akhkhar) - nominative (marfu') | My religion |

#### Grammar Notes
- This verse employs two nominal sentences with inverted word order (predicate before subject).
- لَكُمْ and لِيَ are prepositional phrases serving as predicates (khabar muqaddam).
- دِينُكُمْ and دِينِ are the delayed subjects (mubtada' mu'akhkhar).
- The inversion adds emphasis and creates a balanced, memorable conclusion.
- Note that دِينِ ends with kasrah (genitive form) due to the attached pronoun, though it's in the nominative position grammatically.

## Grammar Summary

- **Negation with لَا:** Used for present/future negation of verbs and nominal sentences
- **Nominal sentences (jumlah ismiyyah):** Subject-predicate structures with pronouns (أَنَا، أَنتُمْ)
- **Active participles (ism al-fa'il):** عَابِد / عَابِدُون used as predicates to indicate continuous action
- **Attached pronouns:** Attached to nouns (دِينُكُمْ) and prepositions (لَكُمْ، لِيَ)
- **Inverted word order:** Predicate before subject (لَكُمْ دِينُكُمْ) for emphasis
- **Repetition (takrār):** Verses 3 and 5 are identical for rhetorical emphasis
- **Parallel structure:** Each verse mirrors the others, creating a balanced, memorable pattern
- **Present vs. past tense contrast:** تَعْبُدُونَ (present) vs. عَبَدتُّمْ (past) shows both current and historical separation

This surah is excellent for beginners learning negation, nominal sentences, and the use of active participles in Arabic grammar.
