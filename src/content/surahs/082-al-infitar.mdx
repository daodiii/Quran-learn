# Al-Infitar (The Cleaving)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 19
- **Theme:** Description of cosmic upheaval on the Day of Judgment and accountability for human deeds recorded by angels
- **Grammar Focus:** Conditional particles (idha), passive voice constructions, interrogative particles, emphasis with inna, attached pronouns

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** إِذَا السَّمَاءُ انفَطَرَتْ

**Translation:** "When the sky breaks apart"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِذَا | idha | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Conditional particle (harf shart) governing time clause | when |
| 2 | السَّمَاءُ | al-samaa'u | س م و | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | the sky |
| 3 | انفَطَرَتْ | infatarat | ف ط ر | Verb - Form VII, past tense, 3rd person, feminine, singular | Predicate (khabar) - past tense verb | it split apart |

#### Grammar Notes
- **idha conditional**: Introduces a future conditional clause describing the Day of Judgment
- **Form VII verb (infatara)**: Reflexive/passive form of fatara (to split) - indicates self-splitting
- Nominal sentence structure with verbal predicate

## Grammar Summary

- **idha conditional chains**: Four parallel conditional clauses (verses 1-4) describing cosmic upheaval, with main clause answer in verse 5
- **Passive voice constructions**: fujjirat, bu'thirat - emphasize action upon creation without specifying agent
- **Form VII and VIII verbs**: Reflexive/intensive meanings (infatara, intathara)
- **inna emphasis structures**: Multiple uses with lam al-tawkid for double emphasis (verses 10, 13, 14)
- **Rhetorical questions**: ma gharraka (verse 6), ma adraka (verses 17-18) - engaging address style
- **Relative clauses**: alladhi khalaqaka (verse 7), ma taf'alun (verse 12)
- **Contrast structures**: al-abrar vs al-fujjar (verses 13-14) - parallel constructions showing opposite fates
- **Active participles as nouns**: haafiziin, kaatibiin, ghaa'ibiin - describing ongoing states
- **Adverbial time expressions**: yawma (verses 15, 19), yawma'idhin (verse 19)
- **Prepositional phrases as predicates**: fi na'eem, fi jaheem - locative predicates with inna
