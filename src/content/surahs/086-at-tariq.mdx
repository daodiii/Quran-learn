# At-Tariq (The Night-Comer)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 17
- **Theme:** Oath by the piercing star, every soul has a guardian, reflection on human creation, and resurrection promise
- **Grammar Focus:** Oath constructions, interrogative ma adra, inna emphasis with lam, kull universal quantifier, in conditional, comparative structures

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَالسَّمَاءِ وَالطَّارِقِ

**Translation:** "By the sky and the night-comer"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - oath | Oath particle (wa al-qasam) | By |
| 2 | السَّمَاءِ | al-samaa'i | س م و | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Object of oath - genitive (majrur) | the sky |
| 3 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - oath | Oath particle (wa al-qasam) | And by |
| 4 | الطَّارِقِ | al-taariqi | ط ر ق | Noun - active participle, masculine, singular, definite | Object of oath - genitive (majrur) | the night-comer |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Double wa-oath**: Swearing by two related celestial phenomena
- **al-tariq**: Active participle from taraqa (to knock/come at night) - the night visitor

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الطَّارِقُ

**Translation:** "And what can make you know what the night-comer is?"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Coordinating conjunction | and |
| 2 | مَا | ma | - | Pronoun - interrogative | Interrogative pronoun (ism istifham) | what |
| 3 | أَدْرَاكَ | adraka | د ر ك | Verb - Form IV, past tense, 3rd person, masculine, singular + pronoun | Past tense verb with object pronoun | made you know |

- **ma adraka**: Rhetorical question emphasizing the magnificence or incomprehensibility

- Creates suspense before the answer

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** إِن كُلُّ نَفْسٍ لَّمَّا عَلَيْهَا حَافِظٌ

**Translation:** "There is no soul but that it has over it a guardian"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِن | in | - | Particle - negative conditional | Negative conditional particle (harf nafy) | not |
| 2 | كُلُّ | kullu | - | Noun - universal quantifier, masculine, singular, construct | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu') | every |
| 3 | نَفْسٍ | nafsin | ن ف س | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite | Second part of idafa - genitive (majrur) | soul |
| 4 | لَمَّا | lamma | - | Particle - exception/emphasis | Exceptive particle (harf istithnaa') meaning "but/except" | but/except |

#### Grammar Notes
- **in... lamma**: Strong negative construction meaning "there is not... except" = "every"
- **kull nafsin**: Every single soul without exception
- **haafiz**: Active participle - guardian angel watching over each person
- This is the jawab al-qasam (answer to the oath)

## Grammar Summary

- **Oath constructions**: Two separate oath sequences (verses 1-3, 11-12) with respective answers
- **Rhetorical questions with ma adra**: "What can make you know?" (verse 2) - emphasis device
- **inna emphasis with lam**: Double emphasis structure (innahu... la-qadir) showing certainty
- **in... lamma construction**: Strong negative-exceptive meaning "there is not... except that" = "every" (verse 4)
- **kull universal quantifier**: "Every soul" - emphasizing universality
- **Passive voice**: khuliqa (was created), tubla (are tested) - focus on object of action
- **Active participles as nouns**: al-tariq, al-thaqib, haafiz, dafiq, nasir - describing ongoing states/qualities
- **Idafa constructions**: Possessive relationships (dhat al-buruj, bayn al-sulb wa al-tara'ib)
- **min za'ida**: Emphatic extra particle strengthening negation (verse 10)
- **ba' za'ida**: Emphatic ba' in negation (ma huwa bi-al-hazl)
- **Maf'ul mutlaq (cognate accusative)**: yakidun kaydan, akidu kaydan - emphasizes action intensity
- **Diminutive form**: ruwaydan - "a little while" - diminishing the time period
- **Imperative forms**: mahhil, amhil - commands to the Prophet
- **Temporal adverbials**: yawma (verse 9) - time specification
- **Prepositional phrases as predicates**: 'alayha haafiz, 'ala raj'ihi qadir - descriptive constructions
