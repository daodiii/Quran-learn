# An-Nas (Mankind)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 6
- **Theme:** Seeking Allah's protection from the whispers of Satan (waswas) - both from jinn and humans. The final surah of the Quran, paired with Al-Falaq as protective surahs (al-Mu'awwidhatayn).
- **Grammar Focus:** Imperative verbs (قُلْ، أَعُوذُ), repeated iḍāfah chains with الناس, preposition مِن, active participles (ism al-fa'il), genitive constructions demonstrating varying levels of iḍāfah, relative clauses with الَّذِي

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ

**Translation:** "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | قُلْ | qul | ق و ل | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Command verb (fi'l amr) - mabni | Say |
| 2 | أَعُوذُ | a'ūdhu | ع و ذ | Verb - Form I, present tense, 1st person singular | Present tense verb (fi'l muḍāri') - indicative (marfū') | I seek refuge, I take refuge |
| 3 | بِرَبِّ | bi-rabbi | ر ب ب | Preposition + noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Preposition + noun (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | In the Lord, with the Lord |
| 4 | النَّاسِ | al-nāsi | ن و س | Noun - masculine plural, definite | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Mankind, the people |

#### Grammar Notes
- قُلْ is the imperative opening, identical to Surah 113.
- أَعُوذُ expresses the ongoing act of seeking refuge.
- The preposition بِـ indicates "in whom" refuge is sought.
- رَبِّ النَّاسِ is an iḍāfah meaning "the Lord of mankind."
- This begins a pattern where النَّاسِ appears in three consecutive iḍāfah constructions (verses 1-3).

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** مَلِكِ النَّاسِ

**Translation:** "The King of mankind"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | مَلِكِ | maliki | م ل ك | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Apposition (badal) to "رَبِّ" in verse 1 - genitive (majrūr) | The King |
| 2 | النَّاسِ | al-nāsi | ن و س | Noun - masculine plural, definite | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Mankind, the people |

#### Grammar Notes
- مَلِكِ النَّاسِ is an apposition (badal) to رَبِّ النَّاسِ, providing another attribute.
- The genitive case matches the preceding رَبِّ.
- This is the second of three consecutive descriptions, each using النَّاسِ.
- The repetition creates emphasis and memorable rhythm.

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ

**Translation:** "The God of mankind"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِلَٰهِ | ilāhi | أ ل ه | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Apposition (badal) continuing from verse 2 - genitive (majrūr) | The God, the deity |
| 2 | النَّاسِ | al-nāsi | ن و س | Noun - masculine plural, definite | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Mankind, the people |

#### Grammar Notes
- إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ is the third and final apposition in the chain.
- All three attributes (رَبّ، مَلِك، إِلَٰه) are in the genitive case, each forming an iḍāfah with النَّاسِ.
- Together they establish Allah's threefold authority: as Sustainer (رَبّ), King (مَلِك), and God (إِلَٰه).
- The triple repetition of النَّاسِ creates a powerful rhetorical effect.

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ

**Translation:** "From the evil of the whisperer who withdraws"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | مِن | min | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | From |
| 2 | شَرِّ | sharri | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Evil, harm |
| 3 | الْوَسْوَاسِ | al-waswāsi | و س و س | Noun - masculine singular, definite, intensive pattern | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | The whisperer, the one who whispers persistently |
| 4 | الْخَنَّاسِ | al-khannāsi | خ ن س | Adjective - masculine singular, definite, intensive active participle | Attributive adjective (na't) - genitive (majrūr) | The one who withdraws/retreats (persistently) |

#### Grammar Notes
- مِن introduces what refuge is being sought from (parallel to Surah 113).
- الْوَسْوَاسِ is an intensive pattern (فَعْلَال) indicating persistent, repeated whispering.
- الْخَنَّاسِ is an intensive active participle (فَعَّال pattern) meaning one who repeatedly withdraws.
- The combination describes Satan who whispers evil suggestions, then withdraws when Allah is remembered.
- Both words use intensive patterns, emphasizing the persistent nature of the threat.

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ

**Translation:** "Who whispers in the hearts of mankind"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | الَّذِي | alladhī | - | Pronoun - relative, masculine singular | Relative pronoun (mawṣūl) - mabni, referring to الْوَسْوَاسِ | Who, that which |
| 2 | يُوَسْوِسُ | yuwaswisu | و س و س | Verb - Form II, present tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Present tense verb (fi'l muḍāri') - indicative (marfū'), verb of the relative clause (ṣilat al-mawṣūl) | He whispers |
| 3 | فِي | fī | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | In, into |
| 4 | صُدُورِ | ṣudūri | ص د ر | Noun - masculine plural, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Hearts, chests, breasts |
| 5 | النَّاسِ | al-nāsi | ن و س | Noun - masculine plural, definite | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Mankind, the people |

#### Grammar Notes
- الَّذِي is a relative pronoun referring back to الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ.
- يُوَسْوِسُ is Form II (intensive/causative) of the root و س و س, indicating persistent whispering.
- فِي صُدُورِ indicates where the whispering occurs - in the hearts/chests.
- صُدُورِ النَّاسِ is an iḍāfah meaning "the hearts of mankind."
- This is the fourth occurrence of النَّاسِ in the surah, maintaining the thematic repetition.

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

**Translation:** "From among jinn and mankind"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | مِنَ | mina | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | From, among |
| 2 | الْجِنَّةِ | al-jinnati | ج ن ن | Noun - masculine plural, definite | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | The jinn |
| 3 | وَالنَّاسِ | wa-l-nāsi | ن و س | Conjunction + noun - masculine plural, definite | Coordinating conjunction + noun (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf ma'ṭūf) - genitive (majrūr) | And mankind, and the people |

#### Grammar Notes
- مِنَ here indicates the source or type: "from among" jinn and mankind.
- This can be understood as:
  1. An apposition (badal) clarifying that the whisperer comes from both jinn and humans
  2. Part of the relative clause, specifying the whisperer's origin
- الْجِنَّةِ refers to the unseen beings created from smokeless fire.
- وَالنَّاسِ is the fifth and final occurrence of النَّاسِ in the surah.
- The verse acknowledges that harmful whispering can come from both supernatural (jinn/Satan) and human sources.
- This creates a bookend: the surah begins with "mankind" (النَّاسِ) three times and ends with it, for a total of five occurrences.

## Grammar Summary

- **Imperative verb:** قُلْ commands the recitation
- **Present tense for ongoing action:** أَعُوذُ expresses continuous seeking of refuge
- **Preposition بِـ:** Indicates in whom refuge is sought (with أَعُوذُ)
- **Triple iḍāfah chain (verses 1-3):** رَبِّ النَّاسِ، مَلِكِ النَّاسِ، إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ - all in genitive case
- **Apposition (badal):** Each divine attribute builds on the previous one
- **Repetition of النَّاسِ:** Occurs 5 times (verses 1, 2, 3, 5, 6) for emphasis and memorability
- **Preposition مِن:** Introduces the source of evil (verse 4) and category (verse 6)
- **Intensive patterns:**
  - الْوَسْوَاسِ (فَعْلَال pattern) - persistent whispering
  - الْخَنَّاسِ (فَعَّال pattern) - persistent withdrawing
- **Relative clause with الَّذِي:** Describes the whisperer further (verse 5)
- **Form II verb:** يُوَسْوِسُ (intensive/causative) emphasizes persistent action
- **Multiple iḍāfah constructions:**
  - بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ (with the Lord of mankind)
  - شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ (evil of the whisperer)
  - صُدُورِ النَّاسِ (hearts of mankind)
- **Coordinated nouns:** الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ (both in genitive after مِنَ)
- **Genitive case dominance:** Most nouns in this surah are in the genitive (majrūr) due to iḍāfah and prepositions
- **Parallel structure with Surah 113:** Both open with قُلْ أَعُوذُ, both use مِن to introduce the evil, both are protective surahs

This surah demonstrates:
- How repetition creates emphasis and memorability
- Chains of iḍāfah constructions (genitive relationships)
- The use of intensive patterns to convey persistent action
- Relative clauses for extended description
- The final message of the Quran: seek refuge in Allah from all sources of evil

As the final surah of the Quran, it provides a complete grammatical review while offering spiritual protection.
