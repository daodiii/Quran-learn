# Al-Falaq (The Daybreak)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 5
- **Theme:** Seeking Allah's protection from various forms of evil - the darkness, envy, and harmful sorcery. One of the two protective surahs (al-Mu'awwidhatayn).
- **Grammar Focus:** Imperative verbs (قُلْ، أَعُوذُ), preposition مِن with repeated usage, genitive constructions (iḍāfah), active participles (ism al-fa'il), relative clauses, conditional إِذَا

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ

**Translation:** "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the Daybreak"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | قُلْ | qul | ق و ل | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Command verb (fi'l amr) - mabni | Say |
| 2 | أَعُوذُ | a'ūdhu | ع و ذ | Verb - Form I, present tense, 1st person singular | Present tense verb (fi'l muḍāri') - indicative (marfū') | I seek refuge, I take refuge |
| 3 | بِرَبِّ | bi-rabbi | ر ب ب | Preposition + noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Preposition + noun (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | In the Lord, with the Lord |
| 4 | الْفَلَقِ | al-falaqi | ف ل ق | Noun - masculine singular, definite | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | The daybreak, the dawn, the splitting (of darkness) |

#### Grammar Notes
- قُلْ is the standard opening command found in many surahs.
- أَعُوذُ is a present tense verb expressing an ongoing or habitual action of seeking protection.
- The preposition بِـ with أَعُوذُ indicates the one in whom refuge is sought.
- رَبِّ الْفَلَقِ is an iḍāfah construction meaning "the Lord of the Daybreak."
- الْفَلَق literally means "the splitting" or "the cleaving," referring to the dawn when darkness splits to reveal light.

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ

**Translation:** "From the evil of what He created"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | مِن | min | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | From |
| 2 | شَرِّ | sharri | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Evil, harm |
| 3 | مَا | mā | - | Pronoun - relative/indefinite | Relative pronoun (mawṣūl) - mabni, second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive position | What, that which |
| 4 | خَلَقَ | khalaqa | خ ل ق | Verb - Form I, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Past tense verb (fi'l māḍī) - mabni, verb of the relative clause (ṣilat al-mawṣūl) | He created |

#### Grammar Notes
- مِن introduces what is being sought refuge from, continuing from verse 1.
- شَرِّ مَا is an iḍāfah construction: "the evil of what..."
- مَا is a relative pronoun meaning "that which" or "what."
- خَلَقَ is the verb of the relative clause, with an implied subject (Allah).
- This verse provides the general category: evil in all created things.

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ

**Translation:** "And from the evil of darkness when it settles"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَمِن | wa-min | - | Conjunction + preposition | Coordinating conjunction + preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | And from |
| 2 | شَرِّ | sharri | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Evil, harm |
| 3 | غَاسِقٍ | ghāsiqin | غ س ق | Noun - masculine singular, indefinite, active participle | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Darkness, dark night (that which darkens) |
| 4 | إِذَا | idhā | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Conditional particle (ḍarf li-l-mustaqbal) - mabni | When |
| 5 | وَقَبَ | waqaba | و ق ب | Verb - Form I, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Past tense verb (fi'l māḍī) - mabni, verb of the condition (fi'l al-sharṭ) | It settled, it penetrated, it deepened |

#### Grammar Notes
- This verse follows the same pattern as verse 2, specifying a particular type of evil.
- غَاسِقٍ is an active participle (ism al-fa'il) from the root غ س ق, meaning "that which darkens" or "darkness."
- إِذَا introduces a temporal/conditional clause.
- وَقَبَ means when the darkness settles deeply or penetrates - the darkest part of the night.
- The combination describes the evil associated with deep nighttime darkness.

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ

**Translation:** "And from the evil of those who blow on knots"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَمِن | wa-min | - | Conjunction + preposition | Coordinating conjunction + preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | And from |
| 2 | شَرِّ | sharri | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Evil, harm |
| 3 | النَّفَّاثَاتِ | al-naffāthāti | ن ف ث | Noun - feminine plural, definite, intensive active participle (Form I with emphasis pattern) | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | Those who blow (feminine plural), the blowers |
| 4 | فِي | fī | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | In, on, into |
| 5 | الْعُقَدِ | al-'uqadi | ع ق د | Noun - feminine plural, definite | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | The knots |

#### Grammar Notes
- النَّفَّاثَاتِ is an intensive active participle pattern (نَفَّاعِل) in the feminine plural.
- The feminine plural form can refer to women specifically or to groups engaging in the action.
- This refers to practitioners of sorcery who blow on knots while casting spells.
- فِي الْعُقَدِ specifies where the blowing occurs - on or into knots.
- This verse seeks protection from harmful sorcery and black magic.

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ

**Translation:** "And from the evil of an envier when he envies"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَمِن | wa-min | - | Conjunction + preposition | Coordinating conjunction + preposition (ḥarf jarr) - mabni | And from |
| 2 | شَرِّ | sharri | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine singular, construct state (muḍāf) | Object of preposition (majrūr bi-l-ḥarf) - genitive (majrūr) | Evil, harm |
| 3 | حَاسِدٍ | ḥāsidin | ح س د | Noun - masculine singular, indefinite, active participle | Second part of construct (muḍāf ilayhi) - genitive (majrūr) | An envier, one who envies |
| 4 | إِذَا | idhā | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Conditional particle (ḍarf li-l-mustaqbal) - mabni | When |
| 5 | حَسَدَ | ḥasada | ح س د | Verb - Form I, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Past tense verb (fi'l māḍī) - mabni, verb of the condition (fi'l al-sharṭ) | He envied |

#### Grammar Notes
- حَاسِدٍ is an active participle (ism al-fa'il), meaning "one who envies."
- The structure parallels verse 3: active participle + conditional clause.
- إِذَا حَسَدَ specifies the moment when envy becomes active - when the envier acts on his envy.
- This completes the list of specific evils to seek protection from.
- The verse acknowledges the real harm that can come from destructive envy (hasad).

## Grammar Summary

- **Imperative verb:** قُلْ commands the recitation
- **Present tense for continuous action:** أَعُوذُ expresses ongoing seeking of refuge
- **Preposition بِـ:** Indicates the one in whom refuge is sought (with أَعُوذُ)
- **Repeated preposition مِن:** Introduces each category of evil (verses 2-5)
- **Iḍāfah (genitive constructions):** رَبِّ الْفَلَقِ, شَرِّ مَا, شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ, etc.
- **Relative clause with مَا:** مَا خَلَقَ ("what He created") - مَا is the relative pronoun, خَلَقَ is the verb
- **Active participles (ism al-fa'il):** غَاسِق (that which darkens), حَاسِد (one who envies)
- **Intensive active participle pattern:** النَّفَّاثَات (intensive/habitual blowers)
- **Conditional/temporal إِذَا:** Used twice (verses 3 and 5) for "when"
- **Past tense after إِذَا:** وَقَبَ and حَسَدَ refer to specific moments/conditions
- **Feminine plural:** النَّفَّاثَات with feminine ending ـَات
- **Parallel structure:** Four مِن شَرِّ phrases create rhythmic repetition
- **Attached pronouns:** No attached pronouns in this surah (contrast with Surah 114)
- **Definite vs. indefinite:** الْفَلَقِ (definite), غَاسِقٍ (indefinite), النَّفَّاثَاتِ (definite), حَاسِدٍ (indefinite)

This surah provides excellent practice with:
- The preposition مِن and its repeated usage
- Genitive (iḍāfah) constructions
- Active participles for describing agents
- Conditional clauses with إِذَا
- The structure of seeking refuge (a'ūdhu bi-)

The grammatical patterns learned here apply to daily supplications (du'a) in Islamic practice.
