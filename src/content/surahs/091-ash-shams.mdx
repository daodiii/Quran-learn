# Ash-Shams (The Sun)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 15
- **Theme:** This surah features one of the longest oath series in the Quran (verses 1-7), swearing by the sun, moon, day, night, heaven, earth, and the soul. It emphasizes that success comes from purifying the soul and failure from corrupting it. The surah concludes with the story of Thamud and their prophet Salih, illustrating the consequences of rejecting divine guidance.
- **Grammar Focus:** Extended oath constructions with وَ (wa), emphasis particle قَدْ (qad), past tense narrative, passive voice constructions, relative clauses, Form II verbs (intensified action)

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا

**Translation:** "By the sun and its brightness"

| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - oath | Not declinable (mabni), oath particle | by |
| 2 | الشَّمْسِ | ash-shamsi | ش-م-س | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Genitive (majrur) after oath particle وَ | the sun |
| 3 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), coordinates | and |
| 4 | ضُحَاهَا | duhaha | ض-ح-و | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite + attached pronoun | Genitive (majrur) in idafa, هَا 3rd person pronoun in genitive | its brightness/morning light |

#### Grammar Notes
- Opening oath by the sun - وَ is oath particle taking genitive
- ضُحَاهَا: brightness/radiance of the sun, from ض-ح-و root
- First in series of cosmic oaths (verses 1-7)

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا

**Translation:** "And inspired it [with discernment of] its wickedness and its righteousness"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa- | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), shows sequence | and/so |
| 2 | أَلْهَمَهَا | alhamaha | ل-ه-م | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Verb in past (mabni), هَا 3rd person pronoun is first object | He inspired it |
| 3 | فُجُورَهَا | fujuraha | ف-ج-ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite + attached pronoun | Second object (maf'ul bihi thani) - accusative (mansub), هَا 3rd person pronoun in genitive | its wickedness |

- أَلْهَمَهَا Form IV takes two objects: the soul (هَا) and the knowledge (فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا)
- فُجُورَهَا and تَقْوَاهَا: moral opposites - wickedness and piety
- Emphasizes human moral agency and knowledge of good/evil

|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

## Grammar Summary

- **Extended oath series**: Verses 1-7 feature one of the longest oath sequences in the Quran (7 consecutive وَ oaths), each with genitive after oath particle وَ
- **Temporal clauses with إِذَا**: Verses 2, 3, 4 use إِذَا to introduce when-clauses qualifying the oaths
- **Relative pronouns**: مَا in verses 5, 6, 7 functioning as ism mawsul (He who) or masdariyya (the act of)
- **Emphasis particle قَدْ**: Verses 9 and 10 use قَدْ to emphasize the certainty of the oath's answer
- **Form II verbs (intensive/causative)**: جَلَّاهَا, سَوَّاهَا, زَكَّاهَا, دَسَّاهَا, كَذَّبَتْ - showing intensified or causative action
- **Form IV verbs**: أَلْهَمَهَا, أَفْلَحَ
- **Form VII verb**: انبَعَثَ (reflexive/passive meaning)
- **Superlative form**: أَشْقَاهَا (أَفْعَل pattern for "most wretched")
- **Idafa constructions**: رَسُولُ اللَّهِ, نَاقَةَ اللَّهِ
- **Connected narrative with فَ**: Verse 14 has five consecutive فَ particles showing rapid sequence of cause-effect
- **Quadriliteral verb**: دَمْدَمَ (د-م-د-م) - four-letter root for emphasis
- **Attached pronouns**: Extensive use of هَا pronoun referring back to النَّفْس (the soul) and other antecedents
- **Contrast structure**: Verses 9-10 present perfect parallelism (success vs. failure, purification vs. corruption)
- **Historical narrative**: Verses 11-15 shift from universal truth to specific example (Thamud's destruction)
