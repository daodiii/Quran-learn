# At-Takathur (Competition in Increase)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 8
- **Theme:** This surah warns against being distracted by competition for worldly possessions and status (takāthur), neglecting preparation for the Hereafter. It emphasizes that certainty of knowledge about death and the grave should motivate different priorities.
- **Grammar Focus:** Emphatic negation kallā (كَلَّا), future marker sawfa (سَوْفَ), emphatic lam (لَ) particles, conditional law (لَوْ) constructions, and repetition for rhetorical effect.

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** أَلْهَاكُمُ التَّكَاثُرُ

**Translation:** "Competition in [worldly] increase diverts you"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | أَلْهَاكُمُ | alhākumu | ل ه و | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Past verb (mabni), subject concealed (huwa), object pronoun (kum) accusative | It distracted you |
| 2 | التَّكَاثُرُ | al-takāthuru | ك ث ر | Noun - verbal noun (Form VI), masculine, singular, definite | Subject (fā'il) - nominative (marfū') | The mutual competition/vying for increase |

#### Grammar Notes
- **alhākum Form IV**: From lahā "to distract, divert" - causative/intensive meaning
- **Delayed subject**: al-takāthur comes after the verb for emphasis on the action
- **al-takāthur Form VI verbal noun**: Mutual/reciprocal form indicating competition between people
- **Root kathura**: "To be many, to increase" - takāthur is competing to have more
- **Pronoun kum**: Second person plural addressing the audience/humanity generally
- **Implicit object**: What you're distracted from (worship, preparation for Hereafter) is implied
- **Opening accusation**: Blunt statement without preamble, immediately confronting the listener

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ الْمَقَابِرَ

**Translation:** "Until you visit the graveyards"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | حَتَّىٰ | ḥattā | - | Particle - preposition (limit/endpoint) | Not declinable (mabni) | Until |
| 2 | زُرْتُمُ | zurtumu | ز و ر | Verb - Form I, past, 2nd person masculine plural | Past verb (mabni), subject pronoun (tum) attached | You visited |
| 3 | الْمَقَابِرَ | al-maqābira | ق ب ر | Noun - feminine, plural, definite | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb) with fatḥa | The graveyards/graves |

#### Grammar Notes
- **ḥattā temporal limit**: "Until" marks the endpoint - distraction continues until death
- **zurtum euphemism**: "Visited" is a euphemism for death/entering the grave
- **al-maqābir plural of maqbara**: Place noun (maf'al pattern) from qabara "to bury"
- **Jarring reality**: After distraction with increase, the final destination is the grave
- **Second person plural**: Directly addresses the audience - "you" have done this
- **Temporal progression**: From worldly competition (verse 1) to inevitable death (verse 2)

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

**Translation:** "No! You are going to know"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | كَلَّا | kallā | - | Particle - negation/rebuke | Not declinable (mabni) | No! / Nay! / Stop! |
| 2 | سَوْفَ | sawfa | - | Particle - future tense marker | Not declinable (mabni) | Will / going to |
| 3 | تَعْلَمُونَ | ta'lamūna | ع ل م | Verb - Form I, present indicative, 2nd person masculine plural | Present indicative (marfū') with ūna ending | You will know |

#### Grammar Notes
- **kallā emphatic negation**: Strong rebuke/warning - "Stop this behavior!" or "No, it won't be as you think!"
- **sawfa future**: Indicates near-certain future - "you will indeed know"
- **ta'lamūn second person**: Continuing direct address to those distracted
- **Implicit object**: What they will know is left unstated for ominous effect - the reality of death, judgment
- **Threatening tone**: Brief, stern warning of coming realization
- **First of four repetitions**: Verses 3-6 will repeat variations of this warning

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ

**Translation:** "Then no! You are going to know"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | ثُمَّ | thumma | - | Particle - conjunction/sequence | Not declinable (mabni) | Then / Moreover |
| 2 | كَلَّا | kallā | - | Particle - negation/rebuke | Not declinable (mabni) | No! / Nay! |
| 3 | سَوْفَ | sawfa | - | Particle - future tense marker | Not declinable (mabni) | Will / going to |
| 4 | تَعْلَمُونَ | ta'lamūna | ع ل م | Verb - Form I, present indicative, 2nd person masculine plural | Present indicative (marfū') | You will know |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Exact repetition of verse 3**: Only addition is thumma at the beginning
- **thumma intensification**: "Then" or "moreover" adds emphasis, making the warning even stronger
- **Rhetorical repetition**: Emphasizes the certainty and importance of the warning
- **Escalation**: Second warning more emphatic than the first
- **Dual warning pattern**: Two warnings before conditional statement in verse 5

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ

**Translation:** "No! If you only knew with knowledge of certainty"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | كَلَّا | kallā | - | Particle - negation/rebuke | Not declinable (mabni) | No! |
| 2 | لَوْ | law | - | Particle - conditional (contrary to fact) | Not declinable (mabni) | If / if only |
| 3 | تَعْلَمُونَ | ta'lamūna | ع ل م | Verb - Form I, present indicative, 2nd person masculine plural | Present indicative (marfū'), condition of law | You knew |
| 4 | عِلْمَ | 'ilma | ع ل م | Noun - verbal noun, masculine, singular, construct state | Absolute object (maf'ūl muṭlaq) - accusative (manṣūb) | Knowledge |
| 5 | الْيَقِينِ | al-yaqīni | ي ق ن | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The certainty |

#### Grammar Notes
- **kallā repeated third time**: Continues the emphatic rebuke
- **law conditional**: Expresses contrary-to-fact condition - "if you knew (but you don't)"
- **ta'lamūn as condition**: Present tense in condition of law
- **'ilm al-yaqīn maf'ūl muṭlaq**: Absolute object intensifying the verb - "knew with certain knowledge"
- **'ilm al-yaqīn technical term**: Phrase indicating absolute, certain knowledge (not speculation)
- **Deleted answer (jawāb)**: The result of the condition is left unstated - "if you knew, you wouldn't be distracted"
- **Implicit critique**: They lack this certain knowledge, which is why they're distracted

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** لَتَرَوُنَّ الْجَحِيمَ

**Translation:** "You will surely see the Hellfire"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | لَ | la | - | Particle - emphasis (lam of oath/future) | Not declinable (mabni) | Surely / certainly |
| 2 | تَرَوُنَّ | tarawunna | ر أ ي | Verb - Form I, present, 2nd person masculine plural + emphasis | Present indicative (marfū') with heavy nūn emphasis | You will see (emphatically) |
| 3 | الْجَحِيمَ | al-jaḥīma | ج ح م | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb) | The Hellfire |

#### Grammar Notes
- **la of emphasis**: Emphatic lam (lam al-qasam or la- for assertion)
- **tarawunna with nūn emphasis**: Verb has heavy nūn (nūn al-tawkīd) suffix for strong emphasis
- **Double emphasis**: Both la- and -nna emphasize the certainty of seeing
- **al-jaḥīm proper name**: One of the names for Hell, from jaḥama "to burn intensely"
- **All will see**: General statement - everyone will see Hell (believers from safety, disbelievers entering it)
- **Shift from conditional to definite**: After "if you knew" (verse 5), now states what will definitely happen

### Verse 7

**Arabic:** ثُمَّ لَتَرَوُنَّهَا عَيْنَ الْيَقِينِ

**Translation:** "Then you will surely see it with the eye of certainty"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | ثُمَّ | thumma | - | Particle - conjunction/sequence | Not declinable (mabni) | Then / moreover |
| 2 | لَ | la | - | Particle - emphasis | Not declinable (mabni) | Surely |
| 3 | تَرَوُنَّهَا | tarawunnahā | ر أ ي | Verb - Form I, present, 2nd person masculine plural + emphasis + pronoun | Present indicative with nūn emphasis, object pronoun (hā) accusative | You will see it |
| 4 | عَيْنَ | 'ayna | ع ي ن | Noun - feminine, singular, construct state | Adverb of manner (ẓarf) or absolute object - accusative (manṣūb) | [With the] eye |
| 5 | الْيَقِينِ | al-yaqīni | ي ق ن | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The certainty |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Repetition with addition**: Repeats verse 6 with added pronoun and 'ayn al-yaqīn
- **thumma escalation**: Emphasizes progression or intensification
- **hā pronoun referent**: Refers to al-jaḥīm (the Hellfire)
- **'ayn al-yaqīn technical term**: "Eye of certainty" - direct visual witnessing (beyond 'ilm al-yaqīn)
- **Progression of certainty**: Verse 5 mentioned 'ilm al-yaqīn (knowledge of certainty), verse 7 'ayn al-yaqīn (eye/vision of certainty)
- **Three levels**: Islamic epistemology recognizes 'ilm al-yaqīn (certain knowledge), 'ayn al-yaqīn (seeing), ḥaqq al-yaqīn (experiencing)
- **Inevitable witness**: Everyone will directly witness Hell, confirming what they should have known in life

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** ثُمَّ لَتُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَنِ النَّعِيمِ

**Translation:** "Then you will surely be asked that Day about pleasure"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | ثُمَّ | thumma | - | Particle - conjunction/sequence | Not declinable (mabni) | Then |
| 2 | لَ | la | - | Particle - emphasis | Not declinable (mabni) | Surely |
| 3 | تُسْأَلُنَّ | tus'alunna | س أ ل | Verb - Form I (passive), present, 2nd person masculine plural + emphasis | Present passive indicative (marfū') with nūn emphasis | You will be asked |
| 4 | يَوْمَئِذٍ | yawma'idhin | ي و م | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state + particle | Adverb of time (ẓarf zamān) - accusative (manṣūb) | That Day |
| 5 | عَنِ | 'an | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | About / concerning |
| 6 | النَّعِيمِ | al-na'īmi | ن ع م | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Object of preposition (majrūr) | The pleasure/blessings/comfort |

#### Grammar Notes
- **thumma continued sequence**: Links this accountability to the previous witnessing
- **tus'alunna passive voice**: Passive Form I - agent (Allah) not stated but understood
- **nūn emphasis**: Heavy nūn emphasizes the certainty of being questioned
- **yawma'idhin**: "That Day" refers to the Day of Judgment
- **'an + na'īm**: "About the blessings" - concerning worldly pleasures and comforts
- **al-na'īm comprehensive**: All forms of worldly blessings, comfort, pleasure, ease
- **Accountability theme**: After seeing Hell, you'll be questioned about how you used blessings
- **Closing warning**: Surah ends with reminder of complete accountability for all blessings received
- **Connection to verse 1**: The takāthur (competition) was in pursuing na'īm - now accountable for it

## Grammar Summary

- **Form IV causative verb**: alhākum (it distracted you) - from lahā "to be distracted"
- **Form VI mutual verb**: al-takāthur - reciprocal form indicating competition between parties
- **Emphatic kallā particle**: Used three times (verses 3, 4, 5) for strong rebuke/warning
- **Future marker sawfa**: Indicates certain future events (verses 3-4)
- **Conditional law**: Contrary-to-fact condition in verse 5 - "if you knew (but you don't)"
- **Maf'ūl muṭlaq (absolute object)**: 'ilm al-yaqīn - emphasizes the manner of knowing
- **Emphatic la- + nūn construction**: la-tarawunna - double emphasis on future certainty (verses 6-7)
- **Nūn al-tawkīd (emphatic nūn)**: Attached to verbs tarawunna, tus'alunna for strong emphasis
- **Passive voice**: tus'alunna (you will be asked) - agent unmentioned but understood (Allah)
- **Temporal adverbs**: yawma'idhin (that Day) - compound temporal marker
- **Iḍāfa constructions**: 'ilm al-yaqīn (knowledge of certainty), 'ayn al-yaqīn (eye of certainty)
- **Technical epistemological terms**: 'ilm al-yaqīn and 'ayn al-yaqīn represent levels of knowledge/certainty
- **Rhetorical repetition**: Verses 3-4 repeat; verses 6-7 repeat with addition - for emphasis
- **Progression structure**: From distraction (1) → death (2) → warning (3-5) → witnessing Hell (6-7) → accountability (8)
- **Thumma sequence particle**: Used four times (verses 4, 7, 8) to indicate progression and escalation
- **Particle 'an with preposition**: Indicates what the questioning concerns ('an al-na'īm)
