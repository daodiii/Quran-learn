# Az-Zalzalah (The Earthquake)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Medinan
- **Verses:** 8
- **Theme:** This surah describes the tremendous earthquake that will occur on the Day of Resurrection, when the earth will reveal its secrets, and every person will see the results of their deeds - even the smallest good or evil will be accounted for.
- **Grammar Focus:** Conditional idhā (إِذَا) constructions, passive voice verbs, mithqāl (مِثْقَال) measurement constructions, and rhetorical questioning structures.

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** إِذَا زُلْزِلَتِ الْأَرْضُ زِلْزَالَهَا

**Translation:** "When the earth is shaken with its [final] earthquake"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِذَا | idhā | - | Particle - temporal/conditional | Not declinable (mabni) | When |
| 2 | زُلْزِلَتِ | zulzilati | ز ل ز ل | Verb - Form IV (passive), past, 3rd person feminine singular | Past passive verb (mabni), condition of idhā | It was shaken/quaked |
| 3 | الْأَرْضُ | al-arḍu | أ ر ض | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (nā'ib al-fā'il) of passive verb - nominative (marfū') | The earth |
| 4 | زِلْزَالَهَا | zilzālahā | ز ل ز ل | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite + pronoun | Absolute object (maf'ūl muṭlaq) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun genitive | Its earthquake/shaking |

#### Grammar Notes
- **idhā temporal**: Indicates a definite future event (when, not if)
- **zulzilat passive Form IV**: Quadriliteral root z-l-z-l made into verb form - passive voice shows the earth is acted upon
- **al-arḍ as nā'ib fā'il**: In passive construction, the object becomes the subject (deputy agent)
- **zilzālahā maf'ūl muṭlaq**: Absolute/cognate object emphasizing the action - "shaken with its shaking"
- **Attached pronoun hā**: Refers to al-arḍ, showing this is the earth's own specific, ultimate earthquake
- **Quadriliteral root**: z-l-z-l is a four-letter root indicating repeated/intensive shaking

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** وَأَخْرَجَتِ الْأَرْضُ أَثْقَالَهَا

**Translation:** "And the earth discharges its burdens"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | أَخْرَجَتِ | akhrajat | خ ر ج | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person feminine singular | Past verb (mabni), tā' tā'nīth (feminine marker) | It brought forth/expelled |
| 3 | الْأَرْضُ | al-arḍu | أ ر ض | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Subject (fā'il) - nominative (marfū') | The earth |
| 4 | أَثْقَالَهَا | athqālahā | ث ق ل | Noun - masculine, plural, indefinite + pronoun | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun genitive | Its burdens/loads |

#### Grammar Notes
- **wa sequence**: Connects this event to the previous shaking - sequential actions
- **akhrajat Form IV active**: "To bring out, expel" - causative of "to exit"
- **al-arḍ active subject**: Here the earth is active subject (unlike passive in verse 1)
- **athqālahā heavy burdens**: Plural of thiql - refers to dead bodies, treasures, minerals buried within
- **Pronoun reference**: hā refers to the earth, indicating these are burdens it has been carrying
- **Eschatological imagery**: Earth giving up everything it has held - the dead, minerals, secrets

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** وَقَالَ الْإِنسَانُ مَا لَهَا

**Translation:** "And man says, 'What is [the matter] with it?'"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | قَالَ | qāla | ق و ل | Verb - Form I, past, 3rd person masculine singular | Past verb (mabni) | He said |
| 3 | الْإِنسَانُ | al-insānu | أ ن س | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Subject (fā'il) - nominative (marfū') | The human/mankind |
| 4 | مَا | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni) | What |
| 5 | لَهَا | lahā | - | Particle + pronoun - preposition + attached pronoun | Preposition + pronoun in genitive place | To it/for it |

#### Grammar Notes
- **qāla introducing direct speech**: Past tense verb introducing quoted question
- **al-insān generic**: Definite article indicates mankind in general, not a specific person
- **mā interrogative**: "What?" expresses shock, confusion, and fear
- **lahā idiomatic**: "What is wrong with it?" - lahā literally "to/for it" but idiomatically asks "what's the matter?"
- **Pronoun hā referent**: Refers to al-arḍ (the earth) from previous verses
- **Human reaction**: Captures humanity's shock and bewilderment at unprecedented cataclysm

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** يَوْمَئِذٍ تُحَدِّثُ أَخْبَارَهَا

**Translation:** "That Day, it will report its news"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | يَوْمَئِذٍ | yawma'idhin | ي و م | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state + particle | Adverb of time (ẓarf zamān) - accusative (manṣūb) | That Day |
| 2 | تُحَدِّثُ | tuḥaddithu | ح د ث | Verb - Form II, present, 3rd person feminine singular | Present indicative (marfū'), answer to conditional (jawāb idhā) | It tells/reports |
| 3 | أَخْبَارَهَا | akhbārahā | خ ب ر | Noun - masculine, plural, indefinite + pronoun | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun genitive | Its news/reports |

#### Grammar Notes
- **yawma'idhin compound**: yawm (day) + idh (then/that time) + tanwin - "on that day"
- **yawma'idhin as ẓarf**: Functions as temporal adverb indicating when the action occurs
- **tuḥaddithu Form II**: Intensive form "to tell, narrate, report" - more emphatic than simple ḥadatha
- **Implied subject**: Verb refers to al-arḍ (the earth) from previous verses
- **akhbārahā its news**: Plural akhbār indicates multiple pieces of information/events
- **Earth as witness**: The earth will testify about everything that occurred upon it
- **Jawāb al-sharṭ**: This is the answer to the conditional begun in verse 1 with idhā

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** بِأَنَّ رَبَّكَ أَوْحَىٰ لَهَا

**Translation:** "Because your Lord has commanded it"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | بِ | bi | - | Particle - preposition (causative) | Not declinable (mabni) | Because |
| 2 | أَنَّ | anna | - | Particle - emphasis/assertion | Not declinable (mabni), governs accusative | That/indeed |
| 3 | رَبَّكَ | rabbaka | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + pronoun | Subject (ism) of anna - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun genitive | Your Lord |
| 4 | أَوْحَىٰ | awḥā | و ح ي | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person masculine singular | Predicate (khabar) of anna - nominative place (mahall raf') | He revealed/inspired/commanded |
| 5 | لَهَا | lahā | - | Particle + pronoun - preposition + attached pronoun | Preposition + pronoun in genitive place | To it |

#### Grammar Notes
- **bi causative**: Indicates reason/cause - "because" or "due to the fact that"
- **anna governs rabbaka**: anna particle causes following noun to be accusative
- **rabbaka direct address**: Second person pronoun ka addresses the listener (Prophet/reader)
- **awḥā Form IV**: "To reveal, inspire, command" - used for Divine communication
- **lahā recipient**: Refers to al-arḍ - Allah will command the earth to speak
- **Divine authorization**: Earth speaks by Allah's permission and command, not autonomously
- **Complete explanation**: Answers why the earth will speak - by direct Divine decree

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** يَوْمَئِذٍ يَصْدُرُ النَّاسُ أَشْتَاتًا لِّيُرَوْا أَعْمَالَهُمْ

**Translation:** "That Day, the people will depart separated [into categories] to be shown their deeds"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | يَوْمَئِذٍ | yawma'idhin | ي و م | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state + particle | Adverb of time (ẓarf zamān) - accusative (manṣūb) | That Day |
| 2 | يَصْدُرُ | yaṣduru | ص د ر | Verb - Form I, present, 3rd person masculine singular | Present indicative (marfū') | They depart/emerge/return |
| 3 | النَّاسُ | al-nāsu | ن و س | Noun - masculine, plural, definite | Subject (fā'il) - nominative (marfū') | The people |
| 4 | أَشْتَاتًا | ashtātan | ش ت ت | Noun - masculine, plural, indefinite | State/condition (ḥāl) - accusative (manṣūb) with tanwin | Separated/scattered [groups] |
| 5 | لِ | li | - | Particle - preposition (purpose) | Not declinable (mabni) | To/in order to |
| 6 | يُرَوْا | yuraw | ر أ ي | Verb - Form IV (passive), present subjunctive, 3rd person masculine plural | Present passive subjunctive (manṣūb) after li purpose, subject pronoun (wāw) | They be shown |
| 7 | أَعْمَالَهُمْ | a'mālahum | ع م ل | Noun - masculine, plural, indefinite + pronoun | Second object (maf'ūl bihi thānī) of passive verb - accusative (manṣūb), pronoun genitive | Their deeds |

#### Grammar Notes
- **yawma'idhin repeated**: Same temporal reference as verse 4, emphasizing that day
- **yaṣduru from ṣadr**: Means "to depart from, emerge from" - people leaving graves/gathering place
- **ashtātan ḥāl**: Describes state/condition - "in scattered groups" (believers/disbelievers, righteous/wicked)
- **li purpose**: Indicates purpose/result - "in order to" or "so that"
- **yuraw passive Form IV subjunctive**: Made subjunctive (manṣūb) by li - "be shown"
- **Double object passive**: Passive of "to show someone something" - they (1st object) are shown their deeds (2nd object)
- **Divine judgment**: People separated by categories to witness the record of their actions

### Verse 7

**Arabic:** فَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ خَيْرًا يَرَهُ

**Translation:** "So whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction/result | Not declinable (mabni) | So/then |
| 2 | مَن | man | - | Particle - conditional/relative pronoun | Not declinable (mabni), condition particle | Whoever |
| 3 | يَعْمَلْ | ya'mal | ع م ل | Verb - Form I, present jussive, 3rd person masculine singular | Jussive (majzūm) as condition of man | He does/works |
| 4 | مِثْقَالَ | mithqāla | ث ق ل | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb) | Weight/measure of |
| 5 | ذَرَّةٍ | dharratin | ذ ر ر | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) with tanwin | Atom/ant/tiny particle |
| 6 | خَيْرًا | khayran | خ ي ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Specification (tamyīz) - accusative (manṣūb) with tanwin | Good [deed] |
| 7 | يَرَهُ | yarahu | ر أ ي | Verb - Form I, present jussive, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Jussive (majzūm) as answer to condition, object pronoun (hu) accusative | He will see it |

#### Grammar Notes
- **fa resultative**: Introduces consequence of the Day's events described above
- **man conditional**: Introduces conditional sentence (shart-jawāb structure)
- **ya'mal jussive**: Made jussive (majzūm) as the condition (sharṭ) of man
- **mithqāl dharratin**: "The weight of an atom" - iḍāfa construction for measurement
- **dharratin smallest unit**: Refers to smallest particle (atom, ant, speck of dust)
- **khayran tamyīz**: Specification showing what type of action - "good"
- **yarahu jussive answer**: Answer (jawāb) to the condition, also jussive
- **Pronoun hu referent**: Refers to the "atom's weight of good" done
- **Absolute justice**: Even the tiniest good deed will be accounted for and rewarded

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** وَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ شَرًّا يَرَهُ

**Translation:** "And whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | مَن | man | - | Particle - conditional/relative pronoun | Not declinable (mabni) | Whoever |
| 3 | يَعْمَلْ | ya'mal | ع م ل | Verb - Form I, present jussive, 3rd person masculine singular | Jussive (majzūm) as condition of man | He does/works |
| 4 | مِثْقَالَ | mithqāla | ث ق ل | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb) | Weight/measure of |
| 5 | ذَرَّةٍ | dharratin | ذ ر ر | Noun - feminine, singular, indefinite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) with tanwin | Atom/ant/tiny particle |
| 6 | شَرًّا | sharran | ش ر ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Specification (tamyīz) - accusative (manṣūb) with tanwin | Evil [deed] |
| 7 | يَرَهُ | yarahu | ر أ ي | Verb - Form I, present jussive, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Jussive (majzūm) as answer to condition, object pronoun (hu) accusative | He will see it |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Exact parallel structure**: Verse 8 mirrors verse 7's grammar completely
- **Only difference**: khayran (good) replaced by sharran (evil)
- **Rhetorical balance**: Equal weight given to reward and punishment
- **Comprehensiveness**: Together verses 7-8 cover all human actions, good and evil
- **sharran opposite**: Direct antonym of khayr, completing the moral spectrum
- **Same accountability**: Evil deeds, no matter how small, will also be witnessed and accounted
- **Final warning**: Surah concludes with sobering reminder of total accountability

## Grammar Summary

- **Conditional structure with idhā**: Verses 1-4 form one long conditional sentence with idhā introducing the protasis (condition)
- **Passive voice constructions**: zulzilat (was shaken), yuraw (they will be shown) - emphasizing the action over the agent
- **Maf'ūl muṭlaq (absolute object)**: zilzālahā - cognate accusative emphasizing the verb's action
- **Form IV verbs**: akhrajat (brought forth), awḥā (revealed) - causative meanings
- **Temporal adverbs**: yawma'idhin (that Day) - compound structure for specific time reference
- **Ḥāl (state) construction**: ashtātan describes the state of people as they emerge
- **Purpose construction**: li + subjunctive verb (li-yuraw) indicating purpose
- **Man conditional sentences**: Verses 7-8 use man + jussive for condition and answer
- **Mithqāl measurement construction**: mithqāla dharratin - iḍāfa showing quantity measurement
- **Tamyīz (specification)**: khayran and sharran specify the type of deed done
- **Parallel structure**: Verses 7 and 8 are perfectly parallel, differing only in khayr vs. sharr
- **Quadriliteral root**: zalzala (z-l-z-l) - four-letter root indicating repetitive/intensive action
- **Direct speech**: qāla introduces quoted interrogative (mā lahā)
- **Bi-anna causal clause**: "Because indeed..." provides explanation for earth's testimony
