# Al-'Adiyat (The Chargers)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 11
- **Theme:** A vivid depiction of war horses charging into battle as a metaphor for human ingratitude and obsession with worldly wealth. Contrasts the vigor horses show in serving their purpose with humanity's failure to be grateful to their Creator. Ends with a stark reminder of resurrection and divine knowledge.
- **Grammar Focus:** Oath constructions with wa- series, active participles (ism fa'il), inna emphasis, fa- connective (causal fa'), idha conditional, relative pronouns, nominal sentences

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَٱلْعَـٰدِيَـٰتِ ضَبْحًۭا

**Translation:** "By the chargers panting"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - oath | Not declinable (mabni), introduces oath | By |
| 2 | ٱلْعَـٰدِيَـٰتِ | al-'ādiyāti | ع د و | Active participle - feminine, plural, definite | Object of oath (muqsam bihi) - genitive (majrur) | the chargers/runners |
| 3 | ضَبْحًۭا | dabhan | ض ب ح | Noun/Masdar - masculine, singular, indefinite | Adverbial (maf'ul mutlaq) - accusative (mansub) | panting |

#### Grammar Notes
- Oath construction with wa- introducing muqsam bihi
- Al-'ādiyāt is active participle (ism fa'il) from 'adā (to run/charge)
- Dabhan is maf'ul mutlaq (cognate accusative) describing manner
- Refers to war horses breathing hard as they run

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** فَٱلْمُورِيَـٰتِ قَدْحًۭا

**Translation:** "And those that strike sparks [with their hooves]"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), connects oaths | And/Then |
| 2 | ٱلْمُورِيَـٰتِ | al-mūriyāti | و ر ي | Active participle - feminine, plural, definite | Object of oath (muqsam bihi) - genitive (majrur) | those that strike sparks |
| 3 | قَدْحًۭا | qadhan | ق د ح | Noun/Masdar - masculine, singular, indefinite | Adverbial (maf'ul mutlaq) - accusative (mansub) | striking/sparking |

- Fa- connects to previous oath (series of oaths)
- Al-mūriyāt is active participle from Form IV awrā (to strike fire/sparks)
- Qadhan is maf'ul mutlaq specifying the manner of action
- Image: hooves striking stones producing sparks in darkness

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** فَأَثَرْنَ بِهِۦ نَقْعًۭا

**Translation:** "And thereby raise up clouds of dust"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), connects actions | And then |
| 2 | أَثَرْنَ | atharna | ء ث ر | Verb - Form I, past tense, 3rd person feminine plural | Verb coordinated in oath series - mabni 'ala sukun | they stirred up |
| 3 | بِهِۦ | bihi | - | Particle - preposition + attached pronoun | Bi = preposition, hi = pronoun (refers to the raid) | thereby/with it |

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** وَإِنَّهُۥ لِحُبِّ ٱلْخَيْرِ لَشَدِيدٌۭ

**Translation:** "And indeed, he is intense in love of wealth"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

### Verse 9

**Arabic:** أَفَلَا يَعْلَمُ إِذَا بُعْثِرَ مَا فِى ٱلْقُبُورِ

**Translation:** "Does he not know that when the contents of the graves are scattered"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | أَ | a | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), introduces rhetorical question | [Does?] |
| 2 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni), connective | [And then/So] |
| 3 | لَا | lā | - | Particle - negation | Not declinable (mabni), negates verb | not |
| 4 | يَعْلَمُ | ya'lamu | ع ل م | Verb - Form I, present tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Verb in present indicative (marfu') | he know |
| 5 | إِذَا | idhā | - | Particle - temporal/conditional | Not declinable (mabni), introduces temporal clause | when |
| 6 | بُعْثِرَ | bu'thira | ب ع ث ر | Verb - Form I passive, past tense, 3rd person masculine singular | Passive verb in temporal clause - mabni 'ala fath | was scattered/turned out |

- A-fa-lā rhetorical question (doesn't he realize?)
- Ya'lamu introduces knowledge clause
- Idhā introduces conditional/temporal clause (Day of Resurrection)
- Bu'thira is passive of ba'thara (to scatter/turn out)
- Mā fī al-qubūr is na'ib fa'il (passive subject): what is in graves

### Verse 11

**Arabic:** إِنَّ رَبَّهُم بِهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍۢ لَّخَبِيرٌۢ

**Translation:** "Indeed, their Lord with them, that Day, is Acquainted"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِنَّ | inna | - | Particle - emphasis | Not declinable (mabni), governs accusative | Indeed |
| 2 | رَبَّهُم | rabbahum | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + attached pronoun | Ism inna (subject) - accusative (mansub) | their Lord |
| 3 | بِهِمْ | bihim | - | Particle - preposition + attached pronoun | Bi = preposition, him = pronoun | with them/of them |
| 4 | يَوْمَئِذٍۢ | yawma'idhin | ي و م | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite + adverbial particle | Adverbial (zarf zaman) - accusative (mansub) | that Day |

- Rabbahum is ism inna in accusative
- Bihim: preposition relates khabir to them
- Yawma'idhin: compound adverb (that Day/Day of Resurrection)
- Khabīr is intensive form (fa'īl pattern) meaning thoroughly informed
- Powerful conclusion: Allah knows all - visible and hidden

## Grammar Summary

### Main Grammatical Constructs

1. **Oath series with wa- and fa-**: Verses 1-5 use sequential oaths connected by fa-

2. **Active participles (ism fa'il)**: Al-'ādiyāt, al-mūriyāt, al-mughīrāt functioning as nouns

3. **Maf'ul mutlaq (cognate accusative)**: Dabhan, qadhan emphasizing manner of action

4. **Adverbial expressions**:
   - Subhan (dawn) - zarf zaman
   - Yawma'idhin (that Day) - zarf zaman

5. **Inna emphasis constructions**: Verses 6, 7, 8, 11 all use inna with la- for double emphasis

6. **Fa- as causal connector**: Verses 2-5 showing sequential actions

7. **Idafa (possessive) constructions**: Li-rabbihi, hubbi al-khayr, mā fī al-qubūr

8. **Rhetorical question (a-fa-lā)**: Verse 9 expecting affirmative answer

9. **Temporal clauses with idhā**: Verses 9-10 describing Day of Resurrection

10. **Passive voice**:
    - Bu'thira (was scattered)
    - Hussila (was made manifest)

11. **Relative pronouns**: Mā fī al-qubūr, mā fī as-sudūr

12. **Nominal sentences**: Verse 6 (inna al-insāna... la-kanūd)

13. **Prepositional phrases as khabar**: Bihim yawma'idhin la-khabīr

14. **Contrast structure**: Verses 1-5 (horses' dedication) vs. verses 6-8 (human ingratitude)

### Pedagogical Notes

This surah is excellent for teaching:
- Sequential oath constructions using wa- and fa-
- Active participles functioning as substantive nouns
- Maf'ul mutlaq (cognate accusative) for emphasis
- The inna... la- construction for strong emphasis
- Passive voice in eschatological contexts
- Rhetorical questions (a-fa-lā pattern)
- Temporal clauses with idhā
- The contrast between physical imagery (horses) and abstract concepts (ingratitude)
- How fa- can show both sequence and causation
- Relative pronouns with prepositional phrases (mā fī...)
