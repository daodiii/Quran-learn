# Ash-Sharh (The Relief)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 8
- **Theme:** This surah consoles the Prophet ﷺ after the hardships he faced in conveying the message, emphasizing that with every hardship comes ease, repeated twice for emphasis. It recounts Allah's blessings upon him.
- **Grammar Focus:** Interrogative alam (أَلَمْ) constructions, inna emphasis structures, prepositional phrase ma'a (مَعَ) meaning "with," and repetition for rhetorical effect.

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ

**Translation:** "Have We not expanded for you your breast?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | أَلَمْ | alam | - | Particle - interrogative + negation | Not declinable (mabni), jussive particle | Have not / Did not |
| 2 | نَشْرَحْ | nashraḥ | ش ر ح | Verb - Form I, present/jussive, 1st person plural | Jussive (majzūm) by alam, subject pronoun (nahnu) implied | We expand |
| 3 | لَكَ | laka | - | Particle + pronoun - preposition + attached pronoun | Preposition + pronoun in genitive place (mahall jarr) | For you |
| 4 | صَدْرَكَ | ṣadraka | ص د ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + pronoun | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun in genitive | Your breast/chest |

#### Grammar Notes
- **alam interrogative negation**: Rhetorical question expecting affirmative answer - "Indeed We did expand..."
- **nashraḥ jussive**: Verb in jussive mood (majzūm) due to alam particle
- **laka indirect object**: Benefactive construction showing for whose benefit the action occurred
- **ṣadr metaphorical**: "Breast" (ṣadr) metaphorically refers to the heart, understanding, and capacity to receive revelation

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** وَوَضَعْنَا عَنكَ وِزْرَكَ

**Translation:** "And We removed from you your burden"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | وَضَعْنَا | waḍa'nā | و ض ع | Verb - Form I, past, 1st person plural | Past verb (mabni), subject pronoun (nā) in nominative place | We removed/placed down |
| 3 | عَنكَ | 'anka | ع ن | Particle + pronoun - preposition + attached pronoun | Preposition + pronoun in genitive place (mahall jarr) | From you |
| 4 | وِزْرَكَ | wizraka | و ز ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite + pronoun | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun in genitive | Your burden |

#### Grammar Notes
- **wa coordinating**: Connects this blessing to the previous one
- **waḍa'nā past tense**: Completed action, indicating the burden has already been removed
- **'anka source preposition**: Indicates removal/separation from the person
- **wizr (burden)**: Refers to the weight of concern and responsibility before prophethood was fully established

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** الَّذِي أَنقَضَ ظَهْرَكَ

**Translation:** "Which had weighed upon your back"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | الَّذِي | alladhī | - | Relative pronoun - masculine, singular | Adjective (na't) describing wizraka - accusative place (mahall naṣb) | Which/that which |
| 2 | أَنقَضَ | anqaḍa | ن ق ض | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person masculine singular | Past verb (mabni), relative clause verb | It weighed down/burdened |
| 3 | ظَهْرَكَ | ẓahraka | ظ ه ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite + pronoun | Direct object (maf'ūl bihi) - accusative (manṣūb), attached pronoun in genitive | Your back |

#### Grammar Notes
- **alladhī relative clause**: Describes and gives detail about the "burden" (wizr) from the previous verse
- **anqaḍa Form IV**: Causative form meaning "to cause to break" or "to weigh heavily upon"
- **ẓahr (back)**: Physical back, metaphorically representing the capacity to bear responsibility

**Translation:** "And We raised high for you your mention"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | رَفَعْنَا | rafa'nā | ر ف ع | Verb - Form I, past, 1st person plural | Past verb (mabni), subject pronoun (nā) in nominative place | We raised/elevated |

- **dhikr elevated status**: Refers to the Prophet's name mentioned in the shahada, in the adhan, and throughout Islamic practice

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا

**Translation:** "For indeed, with hardship [comes] ease"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction/sequence | Not declinable (mabni) | So/then/for |
| 2 | إِنَّ | inna | - | Particle - emphasis/assertion | Not declinable (mabni), governs accusative noun | Indeed/verily |
| 3 | مَعَ | ma'a | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) in accusative (manṣūb) position (adverb of accompaniment) | With |
| 4 | الْعُسْرِ | al-'usri | ع س ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Object of preposition (majrūr) after ma'a, genitive with kasra | The hardship |
| 5 | يُسْرًا | yusran | ي س ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar) of inna - accusative (manṣūb) with tanwin | Ease |

#### Grammar Notes
- **fa resultative**: Introduces conclusion after the blessings enumerated in verses 1-4
- **inna emphasis particle**: Governs the following noun (or in this case, prepositional phrase) as subject, with khabar following
- **ma'a accompaniment**: "With" indicates simultaneous presence, not sequence
- **al-'usri definite**: The definite article makes it "the hardship" (specific known difficulty)
- **yusran indefinite**: Indefinite "ease" (any ease, general ease) - scholars note this means one hardship brings multiple eases

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** إِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا

**Translation:** "Indeed, with hardship [comes] ease"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِنَّ | inna | - | Particle - emphasis/assertion | Not declinable (mabni), governs accusative noun | Indeed/verily |
| 2 | مَعَ | ma'a | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) in accusative (manṣūb) position | With |
| 3 | الْعُسْرِ | al-'usri | ع س ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Object of preposition (majrūr) after ma'a, genitive with kasra | The hardship |
| 4 | يُسْرًا | yusran | ي س ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar) of inna - accusative (manṣūb) with tanwin | Ease |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Exact repetition**: Verse 6 is identical to verse 5, emphasizing the certainty and importance of this promise
- **Rhetorical repetition**: Emphasizes and confirms the promise - scholars say "one hardship cannot overcome two eases"
- **Same grammatical structure**: inna + prepositional phrase (subject) + indefinite noun (predicate)

### Verse 7

**Arabic:** فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَانصَبْ

**Translation:** "So when you have finished [your duties], then stand up [for worship]"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | So/then |
| 2 | إِذَا | idhā | - | Particle - conditional/temporal | Not declinable (mabni), governs following verb | When/whenever |
| 3 | فَرَغْتَ | faraghta | ف ر غ | Verb - Form I, past, 2nd person masculine singular | Past verb (mabni), subject pronoun (anta) attached, condition of idhā | You finished/became free |
| 4 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction/answer | Not declinable (mabni), introduces answer to conditional | Then |
| 5 | انصَبْ | inṣab | ن ص ب | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Imperative (jussive in form), implicit subject (anta) | Stand up/exert yourself |

#### Grammar Notes
- **idhā conditional**: Temporal/conditional particle indicating "when" (definite future occurrence)
- **faraghta protasis**: The "if/when" clause (condition) of the conditional sentence
- **fa-inṣab apodosis**: The "then" clause (result), introduced by fa
- **inṣab imperative**: Command form directed to the Prophet ﷺ

### Verse 8

**Arabic:** وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَارْغَب

**Translation:** "And to your Lord direct [your] longing"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | إِلَىٰ | ilā | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | To/toward |
| 3 | رَبِّكَ | rabbika | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + pronoun | Object of preposition (majrūr), attached pronoun in genitive | Your Lord |
| 4 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction/emphasis | Not declinable (mabni) | So/then |
| 5 | ارْغَب | irgḥab | ر غ ب | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person masculine singular | Imperative (jussive in form), implicit subject (anta) | Direct your desire/seek |

#### Grammar Notes
- **ilā rabbika fronted**: Prepositional phrase placed before the verb for emphasis - "to your Lord specifically"
- **fa emphasis**: Emphasizes the command, indicating consequence or conclusion
- **irgḥab imperative**: Command to turn in devotion and longing exclusively to Allah
- **Parallel structure with verse 7**: Both verses contain fa + imperative, giving two connected commands

## Grammar Summary

- **Rhetorical interrogative with alam**: Used to affirm rather than question, creates emphasis
- **Past tense Divine actions**: Three past tense verbs (nashraḥ, waḍa'nā, rafa'nā) enumerate completed blessings
- **Inna emphasis structures**: Used twice (verses 5-6) to emphasize the promise of ease with hardship
- **Ma'a preposition**: Indicates accompaniment/simultaneity, not sequence - "with" not "after"
- **Repetition for emphasis**: Verse 6 repeats verse 5 exactly, doubling the assurance
- **Conditional sentence with idhā**: Verse 7 uses temporal/conditional to give instruction for when tasks are complete
- **Imperative mood commands**: Two commands (inṣab, irgḥab) give practical application of the surah's message
- **Fronted prepositional phrase**: "ilā rabbika" placed first in verse 8 for rhetorical emphasis on the direction of devotion
