# Al-Qadr (The Night of Decree)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 5
- **Theme:** This surah describes the profound significance of Laylat al-Qadr (the Night of Decree/Power), the night when the Quran began to be revealed. It emphasizes that this one night is better than a thousand months, and describes the peaceful, blessed nature of the night.
- **Grammar Focus:** Inna emphasis constructions, interrogative mā for emphasis/wonder, comparative khayr min (خَيْرٌ مِنْ) structures, and prepositional phrase min (مِنْ) for source/origin.

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** إِنَّا أَنزَلْنَاهُ فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ

**Translation:** "Indeed, We sent it down during the Night of Decree"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِنَّا | innā | - | Particle + pronoun - emphasis particle + attached pronoun | Not declinable (mabni), governs accusative, pronoun is subject (ism innā) | Indeed We |
| 2 | أَنزَلْنَاهُ | anzalnāhu | ن ز ل | Verb - Form IV, past, 1st person plural + pronoun | Predicate (khabar) of innā - nominative place (mahall raf'), object pronoun (hu) accusative | We sent it down |
| 3 | فِي | fī | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | In/during |
| 4 | لَيْلَةِ | laylati | ل ي ل | Noun - feminine, singular, construct state | Object of preposition (majrūr), first part of iḍāfa | Night |
| 5 | الْقَدْرِ | al-qadri | ق د ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The decree/power/majesty |

#### Grammar Notes
- **innā emphasis**: Inna governs accusative case for subject; here pronoun nā is fused with inna
- **anzalnāhu Form IV**: "To send down" - used specifically for revelation from Allah
- **Pronoun hu referent**: Refers to the Quran (though not explicitly mentioned until understood from context)
- **laylat al-qadr iḍāfa**: "Night of the Qadr" - qadr means decree, destiny, power, or honor
- **Historical context**: References the night when revelation of the Quran began (in Ramadan)

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ

**Translation:** "And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 2 | مَا | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni) | What |
| 3 | أَدْرَاكَ | adrāka | د ر ي | Verb - Form IV, past, 3rd person masculine singular + pronoun | Past verb (mabni), subject concealed (huwa), object pronoun (ka) accusative | It made you know |
| 4 | مَا | mā | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), subject (mubtada') | What |
| 5 | لَيْلَةُ | laylatu | ل ي ل | Noun - feminine, singular, construct state | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfū') | Night |
| 6 | الْقَدْرِ | al-qadri | ق د ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The decree |

#### Grammar Notes
- **wa mā adrāka mā formula**: Standard Quranic rhetorical device to emphasize magnitude/importance
- **adrāka Form IV**: "To cause to know" - rhetorical, implying "You cannot fully comprehend..."
- **First mā interrogative**: Begins the rhetorical question about knowledge
- **Second mā interrogative**: Begins embedded question (direct object clause): "what is Laylat al-Qadr?"
- **Double question structure**: "What made you know [what Laylat al-Qadr is]?" = "How could you possibly know the magnitude of this night?"
- **Rhetorical purpose**: Sets up verse 3's answer, building anticipation

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ

**Translation:** "The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | لَيْلَةُ | laylatu | ل ي ل | Noun - feminine, singular, construct state | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfū') | Night |
| 2 | الْقَدْرِ | al-qadri | ق د ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The decree |
| 3 | خَيْرٌ | khayrun | خ ي ر | Noun - elative/comparative, masculine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar) - nominative (marfū') with tanwin | Better/more excellent |
| 4 | مِنْ | min | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | Than/from |
| 5 | أَلْفِ | alfi | أ ل ف | Noun - number, masculine, singular, construct state | Object of preposition (majrūr) | Thousand |
| 6 | شَهْرٍ | shahrin | ش ه ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) with tanwin | Month |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Nominal sentence**: Simple subject-predicate structure (mubtada'-khabar)
- **khayr comparative**: Elative adjective meaning "better" when followed by min
- **min comparative particle**: "Than" - introduces the standard of comparison
- **alf shahr iḍāfa**: "Thousand months" - approximately 83 years
- **Mathematical emphasis**: One night equals more than an entire human lifetime of worship
- **Reward implication**: Worship during this night brings reward exceeding 1000 months of worship

### Verse 4

**Arabic:** تَنَزَّلُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍ

**Translation:** "The angels and the Spirit descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | تَنَزَّلُ | tanazzalu | ن ز ل | Verb - Form V, present, 3rd person feminine singular | Present indicative (marfū') | Descend/come down |
| 2 | الْمَلَائِكَةُ | al-malā'ikatu | م ل ك | Noun - masculine, plural, definite | Subject (fā'il) - nominative (marfū') | The angels |
| 3 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Not declinable (mabni) | And |
| 4 | الرُّوحُ | al-rūḥu | ر و ح | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Conjoined subject - nominative (marfū') | The Spirit |
| 5 | فِيهَا | fīhā | - | Particle + pronoun - preposition + attached pronoun | Preposition + pronoun in genitive place (mahall jarr) | In it/therein |
| 6 | بِ | bi | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | With/by |
| 7 | إِذْنِ | idhni | أ ذ ن | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state | Object of preposition (majrūr) | Permission |
| 8 | رَبِّهِم | rabbihim | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + pronoun | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr), attached pronoun genitive | Their Lord |
| 9 | مِن | min | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni) | For/concerning |

| 11 | أَمْرٍ | amrin | أ م ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) with tanwin | Matter/affair/command |

#### Grammar Notes
- **tanazzalu Form V**: Reflexive/intensive form of "to descend" - gradual, ceremonial descent
- **al-malā'ika subject**: Broken plural of malak (angel)
- **al-rūḥ identity**: Commonly interpreted as Jibrīl (Gabriel), or a special category of angel, or the spirit/soul in general
- **fīhā temporal/locative**: Refers back to "this night" (Laylat al-Qadr)
- **bi-idhni rabbihim**: "By their Lord's permission" - emphasizes nothing happens except by Allah's will
- **min kulli amr**: "For every matter" - angels descend carrying decrees for the coming year
- **Annual decree concept**: This night is when yearly matters are decreed and assigned to angels to execute

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** سَلَامٌ هِيَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ الْفَجْرِ

**Translation:** "Peace it is until the emergence of dawn"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | سَلَامٌ | salāmun | س ل م | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Predicate (khabar muqaddam) - nominative (marfū') with tanwin | Peace |
| 2 | هِيَ | hiya | - | Pronoun - detached, 3rd person feminine singular | Subject (mubtada' mu'akhkhar) - nominative place (mahall raf') | It |
| 3 | حَتَّىٰ | ḥattā | - | Particle - preposition (limit/endpoint) | Not declinable (mabni) | Until |
| 4 | مَطْلَعِ | maṭla'i | ط ل ع | Noun - place/time noun, masculine, singular, construct state | Object of preposition (majrūr) | Rising/emergence |
| 5 | الْفَجْرِ | al-fajri | ف ج ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Second part of iḍāfa - genitive (majrūr) | The dawn |

#### Grammar Notes
- **Fronted predicate**: salām placed before subject (hiya) for emphasis - "It is peace [itself]"
- **salām indefinite**: Emphatic indefinite - "pure peace" or "peace entirely"
- **hiya pronoun**: Refers to Laylat al-Qadr
- **ḥattā temporal limit**: "Until" marks the endpoint of this peaceful state
- **maṭla' al-fajr**: "The rising of dawn" - when Fajr prayer time begins, the special night ends
- **Peaceful atmosphere**: The entire night is characterized by safety, blessings, and tranquility
- **Time span**: From sunset to dawn - the complete night is blessed

## Grammar Summary

- **Inna emphasis particle**: Opens the surah with innā to emphasize the statement about revelation
- **Form IV verbs**: anzalnā (to send down), adrāka (to make know) - causative forms indicating Divine action
- **Rhetorical question formula**: wa mā adrāka mā... - Quranic pattern to express wonder and magnitude
- **Comparative structure**: khayr min (better than) - elative adjective with min preposition
- **Iḍāfa constructions**: laylat al-qadr (Night of Decree), idhni rabbihim (their Lord's permission), kulli amr (every matter), maṭla' al-fajr (emergence of dawn)
- **Form V reflexive**: tanazzalu - emphasizes the deliberate, ceremonial nature of angelic descent
- **Fronted predicate**: salāmun hiya - predicate before subject for rhetorical emphasis
- **Temporal prepositions**: fī (in/during), ḥattā (until) - mark the time span of the blessed night
- **Prepositional phrases**: Multiple uses of min, bi, fī showing relationships of source, instrumentality, and location
- **Pronoun reference**: Consistent use of pronouns (hu, hā, hiya) referring back to previously mentioned entities
