# Al-A'la (The Most High)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 19
- **Theme:** Command to glorify Allah, His creation and guidance, ease and remembrance, success through purification, and reference to earlier scriptures
- **Grammar Focus:** Imperative forms (sabbih), relative clauses with alladhi, comparative/superlative forms (a'la), future tense constructions, adversative bal, negative particles

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الْأَعْلَىٰ

**Translation:** "Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | سَبِّحِ | sabbih | س ب ح | Verb - Form II, imperative, 2nd person, masculine, singular | Imperative verb - majzum (understood) | glorify |
| 2 | اسْمَ | isma | س م و | Noun - masculine, singular, construct state | Object (maf'ul bihi) - accusative (mansub) | the name |
| 3 | رَبِّكَ | rabbika | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + pronoun | Second part of idafa - genitive (majrur) + possessive | your Lord |
| 4 | الْأَعْلَىٰ | al-a'laa | ع ل و | Noun - elative/superlative, masculine, singular, definite | Adjective (na't) - genitive (majrur) | the Most High |

#### Grammar Notes
- **sabbih**: Form II imperative - command to glorify/exalt
- **ism rabbika**: Idafa construction - "the name of your Lord" = Allah and His attributes
- **al-a'la**: Superlative form (ism al-tafdil) - the Highest, Supreme
- Opening with command establishes surah's theme: exaltation of Allah

### Verse 6

**Arabic:** سَنُقْرِئُكَ فَلَا تَنسَىٰ

**Translation:** "We will make you recite, and you will not forget"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | سَ | sa | - | Particle - future | Future prefix (seen al-istiqbal) | will |
| 2 | نُقْرِئُكَ | nuqri'uka | ق ر أ | Verb - Form IV, present tense, 1st person, plural + pronoun | Future verb with object pronoun - indicative (marfu') | We make you recite |

- Shift from 3rd person (describing Allah) to 1st person plural (Allah speaking directly)

## Grammar Summary

- **Imperative forms**: sabbih (glorify), dhakkir (remind) - direct commands opening and structuring surah
- **Relative clauses with alladhi**: Multiple uses describing Allah's attributes and actions (verses 2-4, 12)
- **Superlative and comparative forms (ism al-tafdil)**: al-a'la (Most High), al-ashqa (most wretched), al-kubra (greatest), khayrun (better), abqa (more lasting), al-ula (first)
- **Sequential fa constructions**: khalaqa fa-sawwa, qaddara fa-hada - showing chronological/logical progression
- **Future tense with sa-**: sa-nuqri'uka, sa-yadhdhakkaru - predictions and promises
- **Form II intensive verbs**: sabbih, sawwa, qaddara, salla, dhakkir - emphasizing intensity of action
- **Form IV causative verbs**: akhraja (brought out), aflaha (succeeded), tu'thirun (prefer)
- **Form V reflexive verbs**: yadhdhakkaru (will remember for himself), tazakka (purified himself), yatajannab (avoid)
- **inna emphasis constructions**: Multiple uses with lam for double emphasis (verses 7, 18)
- **Adversative bal**: Shifting focus (verse 16) from positive example to criticism
- **Negative particles**: la (verses 6, 13), la... wa la (neither... nor) - verse 13
- **Conditional constructions**: in nafa'at (if it benefits) - verse 9
- **Relative pronouns**: man (whoever) - conditional/general relative (verses 10, 14)
- **Idafa constructions**: ism rabbika, al-hayat al-dunya, suhuf Ibrahim wa Musa
- **Badal (appositive)**: Explaining and specifying (verse 19 explains verse 18)
- **Merisms (opposite pairs)**: al-jahr wa ma yakhfa (open and hidden), la yamut wa la yahya (neither die nor live)
- **Play on words from same root**: yassara/yusra (ease), dhakkir/dhikra (remind/reminder)
- **Demonstrative pronoun**: hadha (this) referring to teachings
