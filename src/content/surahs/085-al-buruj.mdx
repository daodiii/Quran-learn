# Al-Buruj (The Great Stars)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 22
- **Theme:** Oath by celestial signs, story of the People of the Trench who burned believers, divine retribution, and the glorious Quran
- **Grammar Focus:** Oath constructions (wa-oaths), passive voice (qutila), relative clauses with alladhina, inna emphasis chains, fa'il active participle patterns

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَالسَّمَاءِ ذَاتِ الْبُرُوجِ

**Translation:** "By the sky containing the constellations"

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

| 2 | السَّمَاءِ | al-samaa'i | س م و | Noun - feminine, singular, definite | Object of oath - genitive (majrur) | the sky |
| 3 | ذَاتِ | dhaat | - | Noun - feminine, singular, possessive | Adjective (na't) - genitive (majrur) | possessing/containing |
| 4 | الْبُرُوجِ | al-buruuj | ب ر ج | Noun - masculine, plural, definite | Second part of idafa - genitive (majrur) | the constellations |

#### Grammar Notes
- **wa al-qasam**: Oath particle - Allah swears by His creation
- **dhat al-buruj**: Idafa construction - "possessor of constellations"
- **al-buruj**: Great stars/zodiac constellations - magnificent celestial order

## Grammar Summary

- **Oath constructions**: Three-fold wa-oath (verses 1-3) with jawab al-qasam (verse 4)
- **Passive voice**: qutila (cursed/destroyed), passive participles (maw'ud, mashhud, mahfuz)
- **Relative clauses with alladhina**: Describing believers and disbelievers (verses 10, 11, 19)
- **inna emphasis chains**: Multiple uses showing certainty (verses 10, 11, 12, 13)
- **Active and passive participles**: shahid/mashhud, haafiz/mahfuz - showing doer vs done-to
- **Badal (appositive)**: Explaining and specifying previous nouns (verses 5, 18)
- **Idafa constructions**: Possessive relationships (dhat al-buruj, as-hab al-ukhdud, mulk al-samawat)
- **Divine attributes**: Multiple intensive adjectives (al-'Aziz, al-Hamid, al-Ghafur, al-Wadud, al-Majid)
- **Adversative bal**: Shifting focus and refuting (verses 9, 19, 21)
- **Rhetorical questions**: hal ataka (verse 17) drawing attention
- **ma... illa restriction**: "Their only fault was..." (verse 8)
- **Circumstantial wa**: "while" construction (verse 20)
- **Pronoun of separation (damir al-fasl)**: huwa for emphasis (verse 13)
