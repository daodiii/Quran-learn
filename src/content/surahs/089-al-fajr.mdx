# Al-Fajr (The Dawn)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 30
- **Theme:** This surah opens with a series of oaths by dawn, ten nights, and other phenomena, followed by warnings about the fate of ancient nations ('Ad, Thamud, and Pharaoh) who were destroyed for their arrogance. It emphasizes the test of wealth and poverty, and concludes with the peace of the tranquil soul.
- **Grammar Focus:** Series of oath constructions with وَ (wa), interrogative particles (هَلْ), conditional constructions with إِذَا (idha), emphasis particles كَلَّا (kalla), narrative past tense verbs, relative pronouns and clauses

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** وَالْفَجْرِ

**Translation:** "By the dawn"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | وَالْفَجْرِ | wa-l-fajri | ف-ج-ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Oath particle wa + noun in genitive (majrur) after oath particle | and the dawn |

#### Grammar Notes
- Opening oath construction: وَ (wa) used as oath particle (harf al-qasam), takes following noun in genitive case
- الْفَجْر is a time noun referring to the dawn period

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|

### Verse 5

**Arabic:** هَلْ فِي ذَٰلِكَ قَسَمٌ لِّذِي حِجْرٍ

**Translation:** "Is there [not] in that an oath for one of perception?"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | هَلْ | hal | - | Particle - interrogative | Not declinable (mabni), introduces rhetorical question | is there? |
| 2 | فِي | fi | - | Particle - preposition | Not declinable (mabni), governs genitive | in |
| 3 | ذَٰلِكَ | dhalika | - | Demonstrative - far, masculine, singular | Not declinable (mabni) in place of genitive after فِي | that |
| 4 | قَسَمٌ | qasamun | ق-س-م | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Subject (mubtada') - nominative (marfu'), with tanwin dammatayn | an oath |

| 7 | حِجْرٍ | hijrin | ح-ج-ر | Noun - masculine, singular, indefinite | Genitive (majrur) in idafa construction, with tanwin kasratayn | intellect/perception |

#### Grammar Notes
- هَلْ introduces rhetorical question expecting affirmative answer
- Nominal sentence with delayed subject (قَسَمٌ) after prepositional phrase (فِي ذَٰلِكَ)

- حِجْر literally means "restraint/intelligence"

## Grammar Summary

- **Oath constructions with وَ**: Verses 1-4 demonstrate the oath particle وَ (wa) governing genitive case, a hallmark of Meccan surahs
- **Interrogative particles**: هَلْ (hal) in verse 5 introduces rhetorical question; أَلَمْ (alam) in verse 6 introduces negative rhetorical question expecting affirmative answer
- **Conditional constructions with إِذَا**: Verses 4, 15, 16, 21 use إِذَا (idha) for temporal/conditional clauses introducing eschatological scenes
- **Emphasis particle كَلَّا**: Verses 17 and 21 use كَلَّا (kalla) to strongly negate/refute preceding claims
- **Narrative past tense**: Verses 6-13 employ past tense verbs to narrate historical destruction of ancient nations
- **Relative pronouns and clauses**: الَّتِي (verse 8), الَّذِينَ (verses 9, 11) introduce descriptive relative clauses
- **Passive voice constructions**: دُكَّتِ (verse 21), جِيءَ (verse 23), يُعَذِّبُ/يُوثِقُ (verses 25-26)
- **Maf'ul mutlaq (absolute object)**: Used for emphasis in verses 19, 20, 21, 25, 26
- **أَمَّا...فَ construction**: Verses 15-16 demonstrate conditional construction "as for X, then Y"
- **Form IV and Form VIII verbs**: Multiple occurrences (ابْتَلَاهُ, أَكْرَمَهُ, أَهَانَ, يُوثِقُ, الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ)
- **Vocative structures**: يَا أَيَّتُهَا (verse 27) for honored address
- **Imperative mood**: ارْجِعِي, ادْخُلِي (verses 28-30) for divine commands to the righteous soul
