# Al-Kawthar (Abundance)

## Overview

- **Revelation:** Meccan
- **Verses:** 3
- **Theme:** Assures the Prophet of Allah's abundant blessings (including a river in Paradise) and commands him to pray and sacrifice, declaring that his detractors are the truly cut-off ones.
- **Grammar Focus:** إِنَّا (inna + pronoun) construction, past tense first person plural verbs, imperative mood verbs, nominal sentence with إِنَّ structure

## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### Verse 1

**Arabic:** إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ الْكَوْثَرَ

**Translation:** "Indeed, We have granted you al-Kawthar"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِنَّا | inna | - | Particle + attached pronoun | Emphasis particle (harf tawkid) + subject pronoun (ism inna) accusative | indeed We |
| 2 | أَعْطَيْنَاكَ | a'taynaka | ع ط و | Verb - Form IV, past tense, 1st person, plural + attached pronoun | Predicate of inna (khabar inna) - nominative position, + object pronoun accusative | We gave you, granted you |
| 3 | الْكَوْثَرَ | al-kawthar | ك و ث ر | Noun - masculine, singular, definite | Direct object (maf'ul bihi) - accusative (mansub) with fatha | al-Kawthar, abundance |

#### Grammar Notes
- إِنَّا is إِنَّ + attached pronoun نَا (We), functioning as subject (ism inna) in accusative
- أَعْطَيْنَاكَ is Form IV verb with dual attached pronouns: نَا (We - subject) + كَ (you - object)
- The نَا suffix on the verb indicates 1st person plural (We)
- The كَ suffix indicates 2nd person singular masculine object (you)
- الْكَوْثَرَ is a proper noun referring to a river in Paradise or "abundance/great good"
- The Form IV أَعْطَى means "to give, grant, bestow"

### Verse 2

**Arabic:** فَصَلِّ لِرَبِّكَ وَانْحَرْ

**Translation:** "So pray to your Lord and sacrifice"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | فَ | fa | - | Particle - conjunction | Consequential conjunction - not declinable | so, therefore |
| 2 | صَلِّ | salli | ص ل و | Verb - Form II, imperative, 2nd person, masculine, singular | Imperative verb (fi'l amr) | pray |
| 3 | لِ | li | - | Particle - preposition | Preposition - not declinable | for, to |
| 4 | رَبِّكَ | rabbika | ر ب ب | Noun - masculine, singular, definite + attached pronoun | Object of preposition (majrur) - genitive with kasra, + possessive pronoun genitive | your Lord |
| 5 | وَ | wa | - | Particle - conjunction | Coordinating conjunction - not declinable | and |
| 6 | انْحَرْ | inhar | ن ح ر | Verb - Form I, imperative, 2nd person, masculine, singular | Imperative verb (fi'l amr) | sacrifice |

#### Grammar Notes
- فَ introduces the consequence: because We gave you abundance, therefore...
- صَلِّ is Form II imperative (from صَلَّى, final alif dropped in imperative)
- The doubled ل shows Form II pattern
- لِرَبِّكَ: preposition + noun + possessive pronoun (for your Lord)
- انْحَرْ is Form I imperative from نَحَرَ (to slaughter, sacrifice animals)
- The نَحْر refers to sacrificing camels/animals by cutting the jugular
- Two commands: worship through prayer and sacrifice

### Verse 3

**Arabic:** إِنَّ شَانِئَكَ هُوَ الْأَبْتَرُ

**Translation:** "Indeed, your enemy is the one cut off"

#### Word-by-Word Breakdown

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | Root | Morphology | I'rab | Meaning |
|---|--------|----------------|------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | إِنَّ | inna | - | Particle - emphasis | Emphasis particle (harf tawkid) - governs accusative | indeed |
| 2 | شَانِئَكَ | shani'aka | ش ن ا | Noun - active participle, masculine, singular, indefinite + attached pronoun | Subject of inna (ism inna) - accusative (mansub) with fatha, + possessive pronoun genitive | your hater, enemy |
| 3 | هُوَ | huwa | - | Pronoun - detached, 3rd person masculine singular | Pronoun of separation (damir al-fasl) - not declinable | he (emphatic) |
| 4 | الْأَبْتَرُ | al-abtar | ب ت ر | Noun - adjective, masculine, singular, definite | Predicate of inna (khabar inna) - nominative (marfu') with damma | the cut off, the one without legacy |

#### Grammar Notes
- إِنَّ makes the subject (ism inna) accusative and predicate (khabar inna) nominative
- شَانِئَكَ is an active participle (ism al-fa'il) from شَنِئَ (to hate/detest)
- The ـكَ suffix is possessive pronoun (your)
- هُوَ here is called damir al-fasl (pronoun of separation) adding emphasis
- الْأَبْتَرُ means "cut off" - one without offspring, legacy, or followers
- Historical context: opponents mocked the Prophet for having no surviving sons
- The verse reverses the insult: they are the ones who will be cut off and forgotten

## Grammar Summary
- **إِنَّا construction**: Emphasis particle إِنَّ + first person plural pronoun نَا (We)
- **إِنَّ governing accusative**: شَانِئَكَ is in accusative case as ism inna
- **Khabar inna nominative**: الْأَبْتَرُ is in nominative case as predicate of إِنَّ
- **Past tense with attached pronouns**: أَعْطَيْنَاكَ showing dual pronouns (We + you)
- **Form IV causative verb**: أَعْطَى (to give/grant)
- **Imperative mood**: صَلِّ, انْحَرْ commanding actions
- **Form II imperative**: صَلِّ with doubled middle letter
- **Consequential فَ**: فَصَلِّ showing logical result of verse 1
- **Prepositional phrase**: لِرَبِّكَ (for your Lord) with attached pronoun
- **Active participle**: شَانِئَكَ (your hater) functioning as noun
- **Attached pronouns**: ـكَ (your/you) appearing multiple times
- **Pronoun of separation**: هُوَ (damir al-fasl) adding emphasis between subject and predicate
- **Adjective as predicate**: الْأَبْتَرُ functioning as main predicate
- **Contrast structure**: Verse 1 (you have abundance) vs. Verse 3 (your enemy is cut off)
